# Future of Customer Support & Success

**Subtitle:** Rethinking the Resolve-Retain-Grow Lifecycle with AI

---

## 1. The Current State

Every customer support & success function runs on the same lifecycle: resolve, then retain, then grow. The Resolve-Retain-Grow process is the operational backbone. And right now, it is broken at every stage.

**Resolve is inefficient.** L1 agents answer the same 200 questions repeatedly — slow, inconsistent, expensive.

**Retain is structurally neglected.** CSMs spend 80%+ of time on reactive firefighting, not strategic account health.

**Grow is reactive.** Churn signals detected only when the customer threatens to leave.

### Key Statistics

| Metric | Value | Detail |
|--------|-------|--------|
| L1 Ticket Volume | 70% | Of tickets are routine, repetitive, and documentable |
| First-Contact Resolution | 45% | Industry average — the rest escalate or repeat |
| MTTR (Mean Time to Resolve) | 4.2 days | Average for enterprise software support |
| Proactive Outreach | <15% | Of CSM time spent on proactive vs. reactive work |

### Traditional vs AI-Native

**Traditional Resolve-Retain-Grow:**
- L1 agents answer the same 200 questions repeatedly — slow, inconsistent, expensive
- Knowledge base is stale: last updated months ago, incomplete, hard to search
- Escalations bounce between teams — customer repeats context at every handoff
- CSMs spend 80%+ of time on reactive firefighting, not strategic account health
- Churn signals detected only when the customer threatens to leave
- Product feedback from support buried in tickets, rarely reaches R&D systematically

**AI-Native Resolve-Retain-Grow:**
- AI resolves 70–80% of tickets at first contact: instant, consistent, 24/7
- Knowledge base self-maintains: AI generates articles from resolved tickets automatically
- Full context preserved: AI summarises history so every agent knows the customer’s journey
- CSMs focus on strategic health reviews and expansion — AI handles operational support
- Churn predicted 3–6 months ahead from usage patterns, ticket trends, and sentiment shifts
- Product feedback auto-extracted and structured from support tickets into R&D-ready insights

## 2. How to Think About the Future of Customer Support & Success

The Resolve-Retain-Grow lifecycle forces specificity. Not “AI will transform customer support & success” but exactly how each stage changes when AI agents handle the operational layer end-to-end.

### The Resolve Stage: From Ticket Queues to Instant AI Resolution

IFS supports 10,000+ customers across complex enterprise software — ERP, EAM, FSM, SCM. Support questions range from ‘how do I configure this workflow?’ to ‘our scheduling optimisation is producing suboptimal results.’ AI agents (like those from Ada, Intercom, or purpose-built with IFS domain knowledge) can handle the first category entirely: pulling from product documentation, knowledge bases, and historical ticket resolutions. For complex issues, AI triages, pre-diagnoses, and routes to the right specialist with full context — eliminating the repeat-yourself escalation loop. IFS Cloud’s unified platform makes this especially powerful: the AI can actually inspect the customer’s configuration and logs to diagnose before a human even sees the ticket.

### The Retain Stage: From Reactive CSMs to Predictive Health

Customer retention at IFS depends on product adoption, operational value delivery, and executive relationship health. Today, CSMs track this through quarterly business reviews and gut feel. AI transforms retention into a continuous, data-driven process: product usage analytics identify declining engagement. Support ticket patterns reveal growing frustration. Sentiment analysis across emails and calls detects relationship cooling. Platforms like Gainsight and Totango aggregate these signals into a customer health score that predicts churn risk 3–6 months before the renewal conversation. The CSM shifts from ‘checking in’ to ‘intervening surgically when the data says to.’

### The Grow Stage: From Annual Reviews to Continuous Expansion

Growth within the installed base is IFS’s most efficient revenue channel. AI makes it systematic: product usage data reveals which modules are approaching capacity or under-adopted. AI generates expansion recommendations with personalised business cases. A manufacturing customer using ERP but not EAM, with rising maintenance costs visible in their operational data, gets an AI-generated proposal for asset management. The QBR becomes a growth conversation backed by data, not a relationship maintenance ritual.

### Resolve-Retain-Grow as a Continuous Loop

In the AI-native model, every resolved ticket trains the knowledge base. Every knowledge base improvement reduces future tickets. Lower ticket volume frees CSMs for retention and growth. Growth activity generates new support requirements that AI handles. The loop is self-improving: the support org gets better the more it runs, without adding people.

### North Star Target

> **From ~400 (L1/L2/L3 Support, CSMs, Support Ops, Knowledge Management, Training) → 200–250 people**
> 
> Customers per CSM: from 15–20 to 40–50
> 
> AI resolves 70–80% of support tickets at first contact without human involvement. L1 support largely disappears. L2 shifts to complex, multi-system issues. CSMs are freed from reactive firefighting to focus on strategic account health and expansion. Knowledge management becomes automated. The support function shrinks in headcount but dramatically improves in resolution speed and customer satisfaction.

### Target KPIs

| KPI | Target | Context |
|-----|--------|---------|
| AI First-Contact Resolution | 70–80% | Up from 45% overall first-contact |
| Mean Time to Resolve | <4 hours | Down from 4.2 days average |
| Support FTE Count | 200–250 | Down from ~400 at IFS scale |
| Proactive CSM Time | >60% | Up from <15% on proactive work |
| Churn Prediction Lead Time | 3–6 months | From reactive to predictive |
| Customers per CSM | 40–50 | Up from 15–20 today |

## 3. Challenging Questions for the Room

These are not abstract thought experiments. They are the outcomes your organisation needs to engineer in the next 12 to 24 months.

### Resolve

- How do we achieve 80% AI resolution of IFS Cloud support tickets — including configuration questions, workflow issues, and module-specific troubleshooting — with zero human in the loop and higher CSAT than human agents?
- What has to be true for an IFS customer to get a diagnosed, contextualised answer to any product question in under 60 seconds — by an AI agent that can actually inspect their system configuration and logs?

### Retain

- How do we make churn prediction fully automated — where AI continuously monitors usage, support patterns, and sentiment across all 10,000+ customers and generates intervention playbooks for CSMs without anyone manually reviewing health scores?
- If AI handles all operational support and proactively flags at-risk accounts, what does a CSM at IFS actually spend their time on — and is the role closer to ‘strategic account executive’ than ‘customer firefighter’?

### Grow

- How do we achieve automatic expansion identification where AI monitors every customer’s product usage and generates personalised expansion proposals with ROI models — before the annual QBR, without any CSM manually analysing an account?
- What has to be true for IFS to increase net revenue retention to 130%+ — driven entirely by AI-detected and AI-prepared expansion opportunities that CSMs validate and execute?

### North Star

- If we go from ~400 to 200–250 in support and success, what happens to the L1 support team — and is this an ethical transition or a talent waste?
- How do we reskill support agents for a world where ticket resolution is AI’s job — and the human role is complex problem-solving, product advocacy, and strategic account management?
- If AI captures and structures every support interaction as product feedback, and feeds it directly into R&D planning — does the ‘voice of customer’ programme even need to exist as a separate function?

## 5. Hands-On Automation Exercises

Do at least one before the session. Do all four if you want to arrive with a working prototype, not an opinion.

### 1. The AI Support Agent Prototype

Export your top 50 most common support tickets (anonymised). Feed them plus IFS product documentation into Claude. Build a prototype AI support agent that can answer these questions. Test it against 20 new tickets. Measure: resolution rate, accuracy, and response time vs. human agents.

**Output:** A working AI support agent prototype with measured performance against real IFS ticket data.

**Tools:** Claude API, IFS documentation, Python/HTML · **Effort:** 60 min

### 2. The Customer Health Dashboard

Pull data for 20 IFS customers: product usage metrics, support ticket volume/severity trends, NPS scores, and renewal dates. Feed it into AI to generate a health score model. Build an interactive dashboard that shows health trajectory, risk flags, and AI-recommended interventions for each account.

**Output:** An interactive customer health dashboard with AI-generated risk scores and intervention recommendations.

**Tools:** Python, Claude, CRM/support data, HTML/ECharts · **Effort:** 90 min

### 3. The Self-Maintaining Knowledge Base

Take 100 resolved IFS support tickets. Feed them into AI and ask it to: extract the problem-solution pairs, generate knowledge base articles, identify duplicates, and flag gaps where no article exists. Build a pipeline that could run continuously on new tickets.

**Output:** A set of AI-generated knowledge base articles from real tickets, plus a pipeline design for continuous self-maintenance.

**Tools:** Claude API, ticketing system export, Markdown/HTML · **Effort:** 60 min

### 4. The Product Feedback Extractor

Export 200 support tickets from the last quarter. Use AI to extract and categorise product feedback: feature requests, usability issues, performance complaints, integration gaps. Structure it as a product roadmap input: prioritised by frequency, severity, and customer tier. Deliver it as an interactive report that R&D can filter by module, industry, and severity.

**Output:** A structured product feedback report extracted from support tickets — ready for R&D roadmap planning.

**Tools:** Claude, support ticket exports, HTML/ECharts · **Effort:** 2 hours

## 6. What to Research Before the Session

Four tasks to ground the discussion in your reality.

### 1. Know Your Ticket Taxonomy

Pull the last 500 support tickets. Categorise them: what percentage are routine/repeatable vs. genuinely complex? The routine percentage is your AI automation opportunity. If it’s above 60%, the business case is obvious.

### 2. Audit Your Knowledge Base

Pick 20 random articles from your current knowledge base. Check: are they current? Are they complete? Do they match how customers actually phrase their questions? The gap between your knowledge base and customer language is where AI creates the most value.

### 3. Explore One Platform

Spend 20 minutes on Gainsight or Ada. Gainsight shows you the future of customer health intelligence. Ada shows you the future of AI-first support. Both are production-grade and used by IFS’s peers.

### 4. Ask the Uncomfortable Question

Ask your head of support: ‘If AI resolved 80% of tickets and predicted churn 6 months ahead — what would our 400-person support org actually look like?’ If the answer isn’t ‘half as many people, twice as effective,’ the transformation hasn’t been imagined ambitiously enough.

> The support organisations that win customer loyalty will not be the ones with the biggest teams. They will be the ones where every customer gets an instant, accurate answer — and the humans who remain spend their time on the problems that actually require human judgement.
