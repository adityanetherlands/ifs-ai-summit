# Future of IT

**Subtitle:** Rethinking the Detect-Respond-Optimise Cycle with AI

---

## 1. The Current State

Every IT operations function runs on the same lifecycle: detect, then respond, then optimise. The Detect-Respond-Optimise process is the operational backbone. And right now, it is broken at every stage.

**Detect is inefficient.** Service desk handles password resets, access requests, and software installs manually — high volume, low value.

**Respond is structurally neglected.** Software provisioning takes 2–5 days for new joiners.

**Optimise is reactive.** Incident resolution involves manual escalation chains and context loss.

### Key Statistics

| Metric | Value | Detail |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Ticket Volume | 70% | Of IT tickets are routine (password resets, access requests, software installs) |
| MTTR | 4.1 hours | Mean time to resolve for standard IT incidents |
| False Positive Alerts | 45% | Of security alerts that require no action |
| Shadow IT | 40% | Of SaaS tools used without IT approval |

### Traditional vs AI-Native

**Traditional Detect-Respond-Optimise:**
- Service desk handles password resets, access requests, and software installs manually — high volume, low value
- Infrastructure monitoring generates thousands of alerts, 45% false positives
- Security team spends 80% on alert triage, not strategic threat hunting
- Software provisioning takes 2–5 days for new joiners
- Incident resolution involves manual escalation chains and context loss
- Cost optimisation is annual — cloud spend reviewed quarterly at best

**AI-Native Detect-Respond-Optimise:**
- AI resolves 70%+ of tickets instantly: password resets, access, software requests — zero human touch
- AI-powered AIOps correlates alerts, reduces noise by 80%, and auto-remediates known issues
- Security AI handles alert triage — humans focus on novel threats and strategic posture
- Software provisioned automatically on hire; deprovisioned on exit — minutes, not days
- AI manages incident context: full history, diagnostic data, and recommended resolution at every handoff
- Continuous cost optimisation: AI rightsizes cloud resources in real-time

## 2. How to Think About the Future of IT Operations

The Detect-Respond-Optimise lifecycle forces specificity. Not "AI will transform IT operations" but exactly how each stage changes when AI agents handle the operational layer end-to-end.

### The Detect Stage: From Alert Fatigue to Intelligent Observability

IFS runs a complex IT landscape: IFS Cloud (dogfooding their own product), internal SaaS tools, development infrastructure, and customer-facing cloud services. Today, monitoring generates thousands of alerts per day — and the signal-to-noise ratio is poor. AI transforms detection: AIOps platforms (Dynatrace, Datadog, PagerDuty) correlate alerts across systems, identify root causes, and suppress false positives. Security monitoring (Wiz, CrowdStrike) uses AI to distinguish real threats from noise. The IT team stops drowning in alerts and starts seeing patterns.

### The Respond Stage: From Ticket Queues to Autonomous Resolution

The IT service desk is the biggest headcount cost in most IT orgs. At IFS, it handles 7,000 employees' requests: password resets, access provisioning, software installs, VPN issues. AI resolves 70%+ of these instantly. Moveworks, Freshservice, and ServiceNow's virtual agents understand natural language requests and execute them: 'Give me access to the A&D Jira board' → AI verifies permissions → grants access → confirms in Slack. For infrastructure incidents, AI auto-remediates known issues (restart services, scale resources, clear caches) and provides L2/L3 engineers with pre-diagnosed context for complex issues.

### The Optimise Stage: From Annual Reviews to Continuous Improvement

IT cost optimisation at IFS — cloud spend, license management, infrastructure rightsizing — is currently periodic. AI makes it continuous: platforms like Zylo (SaaS management) identify unused licenses and recommend consolidation. Cloud cost tools (Spot by NetApp, Cloudability) rightsize resources in real-time. Security posture is continuously assessed and improved. The IT org shifts from maintaining the status quo to continuously optimising the technology landscape.

### Detect-Respond-Optimise as a Continuous Loop

In the AI-native model, detection feeds automated response, response outcomes train better detection, and continuous optimisation reduces both incidents and costs. The IT org becomes self-healing: common problems are resolved before users notice them. The role of IT shifts from 'keeping the lights on' to 'enabling the business' — because the lights keep themselves on.

### North Star Target

> **From ~200 (Infrastructure, Security, Service Desk, IT Ops, Cloud Ops, Networking, App Support) → 100–120 people**
> 
> IT staff per 1,000 employees: from 29 to 15
> 
> AI resolves 70%+ of IT tickets at first contact. Infrastructure monitoring becomes autonomous. Security operations shift from alert triage to strategic threat hunting. The IT service desk — the largest headcount component — is largely automated. The survivors are cloud architects, security engineers, and IT strategists.

### Target KPIs

| KPI | Target | Context |
|-----|--------|---------|
| AI Ticket Resolution | 70%+ | Of IT tickets resolved without human |
| MTTR | <30 min | Down from 4.1 hours |
| IT FTE Count | 100–120 | Down from ~200 at IFS scale |
| Alert Noise Reduction | 80% | False positive suppression via AIOps |
| Provisioning Time | <1 hour | Down from 2–5 days for new joiner setup |
| Cloud Cost Optimisation | Continuous | From annual review to real-time rightsizing |

## 3. Challenging Questions for the Room

These are not abstract thought experiments. They are the outcomes your organisation needs to engineer in the next 12 to 24 months.

### Detect
- How do we achieve a state where IFS's entire IT infrastructure is monitored by AI that auto-correlates alerts, identifies root causes, and predicts failures before they impact users — with zero human monitoring dashboards?
- What has to be true for IFS's security operations to shift from 80% alert triage to 80% strategic threat hunting — because AI handles all routine security monitoring and incident classification?

### Respond
- How do we make every standard IT request — access, software, password, VPN, hardware — resolved by AI within minutes, with zero service desk agent in the loop for any request that follows a documented pattern?
- If AI auto-remediates 80% of infrastructure incidents (restart, scale, failover, cache clear) before a human even sees the alert, what does the IT ops team actually do — and is the answer 'architecture and strategic projects'?

### Optimise
- How do we achieve continuous IT cost optimisation — where AI rightsizes cloud instances, reclaims unused licenses, and consolidates redundant SaaS tools automatically — without any analyst running a quarterly spend review?
- What has to be true for IFS to know the total cost of its IT landscape in real-time — including shadow IT — and automatically enforce governance without manual discovery audits?

### North Star
- If we go from ~200 to 100–120 in IT, what happens to the service desk team — and is this a reskilling opportunity into cloud engineering and security, or a structural reduction?
- How do we ensure that halving the IT team doesn't create a single-point-of-failure risk — where the remaining team is too small to handle a major incident?
- If IFS's internal IT runs almost autonomously on AI, does this become a case study we can share with customers — 'We run our own operations this way, and here's the result'?

## 5. Hands-On Automation Exercises

Do at least one before the session. Do all four if you want to arrive with a working prototype, not an opinion.

### Exercise 1: The AI Service Desk Prototype
Export your top 100 IT tickets (anonymised). Build an AI agent using Claude that can resolve the routine ones: access requests, software installs, password resets, VPN troubleshooting. Test it against 20 new tickets. Measure resolution rate and time vs. your current service desk.
- **Output:** A working AI IT service desk prototype with measured performance against real ticket data.
- **Tools:** Claude API, IT ticket data, Python/HTML · **Effort:** 60 min

### Exercise 2: The SaaS Discovery & Optimisation Report
Pull a list of all SaaS tools IFS pays for (from procurement/finance data). Cross-reference with actual usage data (SSO logins, license allocation). Use AI to identify: unused licenses, redundant tools, shadow IT candidates, and consolidation opportunities. Build an interactive report showing potential annual savings.
- **Output:** An interactive SaaS optimisation dashboard showing waste, redundancy, and savings opportunities across IFS's tool landscape.
- **Tools:** Python, Claude, procurement data, HTML/ECharts · **Effort:** 90 min

### Exercise 3: The Auto-Provisioning Workflow
Build an automated new-joiner IT provisioning workflow. Trigger: a new hire record in the HRIS. Actions: create email account, provision access to standard tools (Slack, Jira, Confluence, IFS Cloud), assign security groups based on role, and notify the hiring manager. Deploy using Make/Zapier or a Python script.
- **Output:** A working auto-provisioning workflow that sets up a new IFS employee's IT environment in minutes instead of days.
- **Tools:** Make/Zapier, HRIS webhook, API integrations (Google Workspace, Okta, Jira) · **Effort:** 90 min

### Exercise 4: The Incident Response Runbook Generator
Take IFS's top 20 most frequent infrastructure incidents. Feed them into AI with resolution histories. Generate automated runbooks for each: detection criteria, diagnostic steps, resolution actions, and escalation rules. Build them as executable scripts where possible.
- **Output:** 20 AI-generated incident runbooks — with executable auto-remediation scripts for the most common issues.
- **Tools:** Claude, incident data, Bash/Python scripts · **Effort:** 2 hours

## 6. What to Research Before the Session

Four tasks to ground the discussion in your reality.

### 1. Categorise Your Tickets
Pull the last 500 IT tickets. Categorise: what percentage are routine/automatable (password, access, software) vs. genuinely complex (infrastructure failure, security incident, architectural decision)? The routine percentage is your AI opportunity — and it's almost certainly above 60%.

### 2. Audit Your Alert Volume
Count the monitoring alerts generated last week across all IT systems. How many required human action? How many were false positives or duplicates? The false positive rate tells you the AIOps opportunity.

### 3. Explore One Platform
Spend 20 minutes on Moveworks or Wiz. Moveworks shows you the future of the AI service desk. Wiz shows you the future of cloud security. Both are production-grade and essential for an IT org at IFS's scale.

### 4. Ask the Uncomfortable Question
Ask your CIO: 'If AI resolved all routine IT tickets, auto-remediated known incidents, and continuously optimised our cloud spend — what would our 200-person IT org actually do?' If the answer is 'build strategic capabilities and enable the business,' you're thinking right.

> The IT organisations that win will not be the ones with the biggest service desks. They will be the ones where infrastructure heals itself, security monitors itself, and the remaining humans spend their time building the technology capabilities that differentiate the business.
