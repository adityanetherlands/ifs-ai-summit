# Future of Marketing

**Subtitle:** Rethinking the Attract-Convert-Amplify Funnel with AI

---

## 1. The Current State

Every marketing function runs on the same lifecycle: attract, then convert, then amplify. The Attract-Convert-Amplify process is the operational backbone. And right now, it is broken at every stage.

**Attract is inefficient.** Content takes 3–5 days per piece — briefing, writing, editing, design, approval.

**Convert is structurally neglected.** Attribution is broken: can’t tie marketing spend to revenue impact.

**Amplify is reactive.** Field marketing runs 200+ events/year with heavy logistical overhead.

### Key Statistics

| Metric | Value | Detail |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Content Production | 3–5 days | Average time to produce a single long-form content piece |
| MQL-to-SQL Conversion | 13% | Industry average — 87% of MQLs go nowhere |
| Campaign ROI Visibility | 30% | Of marketers who can attribute revenue to specific campaigns |
| Personalisation Gap | 71% | Of buyers expect personalisation; 76% frustrated when they don’t get it |

### Traditional vs AI-Native

**Traditional Attract-Convert-Amplify:**
- Content takes 3–5 days per piece — briefing, writing, editing, design, approval
- Campaigns planned quarterly, optimised monthly — too slow for market signals
- Generic messaging across all 6 IFS verticals — one-size-fits-none
- Attribution is broken: can’t tie marketing spend to revenue impact
- Field marketing runs 200+ events/year with heavy logistical overhead
- Brand consistency enforced manually through review processes

**AI-Native Attract-Convert-Amplify:**
- AI produces first-draft content in minutes — humans edit, brand-check, and refine
- Campaigns dynamically optimised daily based on engagement signals and intent data
- Hyper-personalised messaging per vertical, per persona, per buying stage — at scale
- AI-powered attribution connects every touchpoint to pipeline and revenue
- Events automated: AI handles logistics, personalised agendas, and follow-up
- Brand voice enforced by AI across all channels automatically

## 2. How to Think About the Future of Marketing

The Attract-Convert-Amplify lifecycle forces specificity. Not “AI will transform marketing” but exactly how each stage changes when AI agents handle the operational layer end-to-end.

### The Attract Stage: From Content Calendars to Intelligent Publishing

IFS markets to 6 distinct verticals, each with its own language, regulatory context, and buying triggers. Today, creating vertical-specific content is expensive and slow. AI transforms this: a single research brief becomes 6 vertical-specific articles, 12 social posts, 3 email sequences, and a webinar script — in hours. Jasper, Writer, and Claude can generate content at vertical depth. The IFS marketing team shifts from ‘producing content’ to ‘curating and elevating AI output.’ SEO becomes AI-driven: platforms like Surfer SEO and Clearscope ensure every piece is optimised before publication. The Chelsea FC and Sri Lanka Cricket partnerships? AI generates dozens of localised activations from a single campaign brief.

### The Convert Stage: From MQLs to Revenue Intelligence

The MQL is dying. Only 13% convert to SQL — the rest are noise. AI replaces the volume game with precision: intent-based targeting (6sense, Demandbase) identifies accounts showing buying signals. AI-personalised nurture sequences adapt to each prospect’s engagement pattern. Conversational marketing (Drift, Qualified) engages high-intent visitors in real-time. For IFS, with complex enterprise buying committees (8–12 people per deal), AI maps the committee, personalises messaging per role, and orchestrates multi-threaded engagement. The metric shifts from ‘MQLs generated’ to ‘pipeline influenced with attribution.’

### The Amplify Stage: From Campaigns to Community Intelligence

IFS’s brand momentum (Gartner Customers’ Choice, 22% ARR growth, the O’Hare billboard Guinness Record) needs amplification that’s faster than quarterly campaign cycles. AI enables real-time amplification: customer wins are turned into case studies within days, not months. Analyst report mentions become social campaigns within hours. Employee advocacy content is AI-generated and brand-consistent. The IFS Connect events become content engines where AI captures, synthesises, and distributes insights across every channel — automatically.

### Attract-Convert-Amplify as a Continuous Loop

In the AI-native model, every customer interaction becomes content. Every piece of content generates engagement data. Every engagement signal informs targeting. Every conversion teaches the model what works. Marketing stops being a campaign factory and becomes a revenue intelligence engine that learns and improves continuously.

### North Star Target

> **From ~150 (Brand, Content, Demand Gen, Events, Digital, Field Marketing, Marketing Ops, Comms) → 80–100 people**
> 
> MQLs per marketing FTE: from ~200 to ~600
> 
> AI automates 60% of content production, media planning, and campaign execution. Content writers shift from producing to curating and editing AI output. Demand gen becomes algorithmic. Events shift from logistics-heavy to experience-driven with smaller teams. The survivors are brand strategists, creative directors, and AI-native campaign architects.

### Target KPIs

| KPI | Target | Context |
|-----|--------|---------|
| Content Production | <4 hours | Down from 3–5 days per piece |
| MQL-to-SQL Conversion | >25% | Up from 13% industry average |
| Marketing FTE Count | 80–100 | Down from ~150 at IFS scale |
| Attribution Clarity | >80% | Of pipeline with clear marketing attribution |
| Vertical Personalisation | 100% | Every campaign personalised by vertical, persona, stage |
| Content Velocity | 10x | Volume of vertical-specific content produced |

## 3. Challenging Questions for the Room

These are not abstract thought experiments. They are the outcomes your organisation needs to engineer in the next 12 to 24 months.

### Attract

- How do we achieve a state where one research brief automatically becomes 6 vertical-specific content assets across 4 formats (article, social, email, video script) — with zero manual writing, only human editorial review?
- What has to be true for IFS’s SEO to be fully AI-driven — where content gaps are identified, articles drafted, optimised, and published without a single marketer in the loop for standard keyword targeting?

### Convert

- How do we replace the MQL with an intent-qualified pipeline signal — where every account entering our pipeline has been AI-scored for buying intent, committee mapped, and pre-nurtured through personalised sequences, without any demand gen team manually qualifying leads?
- If AI personalises every touchpoint per vertical, per persona, and per buying stage, what does the demand generation team actually do — and is the answer ‘strategic campaign architecture’ or ‘nothing the AI can’t handle’?

### Amplify

- How do we make every IFS customer win an automatic content engine — where AI generates the case study, social posts, email sequence, and sales enablement asset within 48 hours of deal close, without any writer or designer involved?
- What has to be true for IFS Connect events to produce 50+ pieces of original content (session summaries, customer stories, thought leadership) within 24 hours of the event — using AI to capture, synthesise, and distribute at journalist speed?

### North Star

- If we go from ~150 to 80–100 in marketing, which roles disappear entirely, which transform, and which are new — and is ‘AI Campaign Architect’ a real role title by 2027?
- How do we reskill content writers for a world where AI produces the first draft — and the human value is in brand voice, strategic narrative, and editorial judgement?
- If AI handles production and personalisation, does IFS’s marketing org become smaller and more strategic — or does it use the freed capacity to 10x content volume and go after new segments?

## 5. Hands-On Automation Exercises

Do at least one before the session. Do all four if you want to arrive with a working prototype, not an opinion.

### 1. The Vertical Content Engine

Take one IFS thought leadership topic (e.g., ‘Industrial AI for predictive maintenance’). Use AI to produce: one long-form article tailored to aerospace MRO, one for energy utilities, one for manufacturing, 6 LinkedIn posts (2 per vertical), and 3 email subject lines per vertical. Measure time vs. what your content team would take.

**Output:** 18 pieces of vertical-specific content from a single brief — with a time comparison against manual production.

**Tools:** Claude, Jasper, IFS brand guidelines · **Effort:** 45 min

### 2. The Intent-to-Pipeline Dashboard

Pull data from your marketing automation and CRM. Build an interactive dashboard showing: MQL-to-SQL conversion by source, campaign attribution to pipeline, intent signals from 6sense/ZoomInfo, and AI-recommended next-best-action per account. Make it update-ready for weekly marketing ops review.

**Output:** An interactive marketing attribution dashboard that connects spend to pipeline with AI-powered recommendations.

**Tools:** Python, CRM/marketing data exports, HTML/ECharts · **Effort:** 90 min

### 3. The Event Content Accelerator

Take recordings or transcripts from the last IFS Connect event. Use AI to produce: session summaries, key takeaways, customer quote extracts, social posts, and a highlights newsletter — all within 2 hours. Compare to how long this currently takes post-event.

**Output:** A complete post-event content package produced in under 2 hours by AI — ready for distribution.

**Tools:** Claude, event transcripts, brand templates · **Effort:** 90 min

### 4. The Prospect Engagement Report

Build an interactive, personalised one-pager that can be sent to a prospect after an initial engagement. Input: their industry, company size, and expressed interests. Output: a branded IFS report showing relevant benchmarks, customer success stories from their vertical, and a curated product overview — auto-generated and ready to send.

**Output:** An auto-generated, personalised prospect engagement report that any marketer or AE can trigger in minutes.

**Tools:** Claude, HTML, IFS case study library, brand assets · **Effort:** 2 hours

## 6. What to Research Before the Session

Four tasks to ground the discussion in your reality.

### 1. Time Your Content Pipeline

Map the last 5 content pieces from brief to published. Calculate total elapsed time and human-hours. Then estimate what percentage of each step could be AI-assisted. The typical answer: 60–80% of production time is automatable.

### 2. Audit Your Personalisation

Take your last 3 demand gen campaigns. Were they personalised by vertical? By persona? By buying stage? Count the number of unique variants created. If the answer is fewer than 6, you’re under-personalising — and that’s exactly what AI solves at zero marginal cost.

### 3. Explore One Platform

Spend 20 minutes on Jasper or 6sense. Jasper shows you the future of AI content at scale. 6sense shows you the future of intent-driven demand gen. Both are production-grade and used by IFS’s enterprise software peers.

### 4. Ask the Uncomfortable Question

Ask your CMO: ‘If AI handled all content production, campaign optimisation, and personalisation — what would our 150-person marketing org actually look like?’ If the answer is ‘80 strategists and creatives,’ you’re thinking correctly. If it’s ‘the same team with better tools,’ the ambition isn’t high enough.

> The marketing organisations that dominate enterprise software will not be the ones with the biggest content teams. They will be the ones where every prospect receives a message that feels handcrafted for their industry, their role, and their moment — because AI made mass personalisation free.
