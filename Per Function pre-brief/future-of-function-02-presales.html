Future of Pre-Sales

What happens when every prospect gets a customised, data-driven demo before a human ever speaks?

1. The Shift

Vivun AI’s largest customer cut their RFP response time from 3 weeks to 36 hours by letting AI generate the first draft from their knowledge base. Same accuracy, 95% less human time. The sales team stopped writing responses and started qualifying them.

Consensus automated demo delivery. Their platform generates AI-narrated, product walkthroughs personalised to each prospect’s role, industry, and use case. Result: 40% more demos completed, 30% shorter sales cycle. The Solution Architect still exists. But they’re now reviewing AI output, not building demos from scratch.

Demostack’s playbook is simpler: clone your product, populate it with prospect-specific data, and let buyers explore in real-time. Companies using this model report 60% faster demo prep and a 25% lift in deal velocity because the demo feels built just for them. And increasingly, it was.

One pattern across all three: the Discover-Demo-Prove cycle doesn’t disappear. It gets compressed. AI handles the 70% of work that is repetitive (requirements capture, demo scripting, POC scoping) and lets Solution Architects focus on what humans do best: strategic deal architecture, industry insight, and navigating complex negotiations. The headcount doesn’t drop as much as the work per person does.

2. Your Pre-Summit Homework

Three things to bring to the session. Not to present, but to think about. Write them down. Short answers are fine. What matters is that you’ve actually wrestled with them.

1. A bold vision for 1 January 2027

If you fast-forwarded 20 months, what does your pre-sales function look like? Not incremental. Drastically different. What’s disappeared? What’s new? What does a pre-sales team even mean? Write the headline version: if someone wrote a profile of your function in January 2027, what would it say?

2. The case for transformation

Why does your pre-sales function need to transform, and why now? Not “AI is important” generically. The specific commercial, competitive, or structural reason transformation is non-negotiable for you. What breaks if you stay as you are? If you can’t articulate this in two sentences, your team won’t follow you.

3. The KPIs that matter in the new era

What are the 2-3 metrics that tell you pre-sales is winning in an AI-native world? Demo-to-close conversion rate? Technical win rate? SE utilisation? Deal velocity? Something entirely new? The old KPIs of RFP response time and demos delivered measure effort. What measures outcome in a world where effort is automated?

3. Directional Nudges: Sense / Decide / Execute

Sense: From Discovery Notes to Structured Requirements

What if every discovery call automatically produced a structured requirements matrix, competitive intelligence, and recommended solution architecture, and no human transcribed a single note?

This is happening now. Conversation intelligence platforms like Gong and Momentum transcribe and analyse every customer call, extract structured requirements in real-time, identify gaps against your product capabilities, and flag competitive mentions. A Solution Architect walks out of a discovery call and has a machine-synthesised brief waiting: customer’s current state, desired state, must-haves, nice-to-haves, and budget signals. All auto-extracted from the conversation.

The structural change: discovery stops being a knowledge-gathering exercise and starts being a strategic insight capture. SAs spend less time assembling information and more time asking better questions. The output of discovery is no longer “meeting notes in a shared drive”. It’s a structured dataset that powers every downstream decision.

Decide: From Manual Scripting to Generative Personalization

What if every demo was automatically scripted, personalised to the prospect’s industry, company size, and role, and every SA could deliver a high-quality demo in under 2 hours of prep?

Platforms like Demostack and Reprise let you clone your product and auto-populate it with prospect data. AI generates the demo script. The SA’s role shifts from “build and memorise 40+ scripts” to “validate and refine the AI-generated narrative.” A new prospect asks for a manufacturing demo? The system spins up an environment with manufacturing data, generates the script, and flags edge cases where the SA should deviate.

This compounds over time. Every demo outcome trains the recommendation engine. Which demo modules drive engagement? Which talking points resonate? Which features move the needle in specific industries? The system learns and gets better.

The structural change: demo delivery shifts from art to system. Not because AI is better than expert SAs. It’s because AI doesn’t get tired, doesn’t forget the customer’s priorities halfway through the call, and can run 10 demos a day instead of 2. Expert SAs remain but operate at a different scale.

Execute: From Guess-Work to Predictive POC Design

What if every POC was scoped based on historical data from 500+ similar deals. Not on what the sales rep thinks is right?

POC scoping is currently art. Some SAs scope tight (short timeline, narrow scope). Some scope wide. Nobody knows which approach closes deals faster. AI changes this: analyse your won deals. Which POC scope (4 weeks vs. 8 weeks, 3 modules vs. 7 modules) correlates with closing? Which success criteria do customers actually care about? Which timelines avoid budget-cycle delays? Generate the POC proposal automatically. Not as a template, but as an AI-derived playbook specific to the prospect’s industry, company size, and deal stage.

Value engineering becomes predictive. Instead of “we think your ROI is X,” it becomes “based on 400 similar deployments, your expected improvement in asset uptime is 18-22%, valued at $2.4M annually.” The data is specific, sourced from your track record, and harder to dismiss.

The structural change: the proof phase stops being a cost centre for the pre-sales team and becomes a deal accelerator. Shorter POCs. Clearer success criteria. Higher conversion rates. SAs spend less time debating scope and more time executing.

The Loop Closes

In the AI-native pre-sales model, discovery is continuous and structured. Demo preparation is automated and personalised. POC scoping is data-driven. Knowledge compounds across every deal. A new SA joining your team doesn’t inherit a folder of old RFPs and demo scripts. They inherit a system that synthesises the collective intelligence of every deal the company has ever run.

Thought Exercise

If your pre-sales function were half the current headcount but delivered twice the deal volume, what would have to be true? Not layoffs. What work disappears? What gets radically faster? And what do the surviving SAs actually do. Is it still called “Solution Architect”?

5. Hands-On Exercises

Do at least one before the session. Do all three if you want to arrive with a working prototype, not an opinion.

Exercise 1: The AI RFP Responder  ·  30 min

Take an actual RFP response from the last 6 months. Feed the RFP questions and your product knowledge base (docs, case studies, prior responses) into Claude. Ask it to generate a complete first draft. Compare to the human version. Calculate the hours that could have been saved if AI handled the first draft and humans only reviewed and refined.

Output: A complete AI-generated RFP first draft with a time-savings analysis and quality assessment.

Tools: Claude, your product documentation, prior RFP responses

Exercise 2: The Discovery-to-Demo Pipeline  ·  60 min

Use a transcript from a real discovery call (or record a new one). Feed it into Claude and ask it to: extract structured requirements, map them to your product modules, identify gaps, and generate a personalised demo script with talking points for each section. Then use Demostack or Reprise to mock up what that demo environment would look like auto-populated with prospect-specific data.

Output: A complete AI-generated demo preparation package: requirements matrix, solution map, script, and a mockup of the personalised demo environment.

Tools: Claude, discovery transcript, Demostack/Reprise demo (or HTML mockup)

Exercise 3: The Prospect Value Model  ·  90 min

Build an interactive value calculator that takes 5 key inputs (industry, company size, current state metric, target state metric, annual spend) and outputs a personalised ROI projection using your benchmark data. Use case studies and public metrics to populate the calculation. Deploy it so you can use it in a customer meeting to generate a live ROI on the fly.

Output: A deployed interactive value calculator that any team member can use during a call to generate a personalised business case in real-time.

Tools: Claude, HTML/JavaScript, your benchmark data
