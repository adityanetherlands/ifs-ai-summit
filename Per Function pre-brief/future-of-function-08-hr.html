Future of HR

Rethinking the Joiner-Mover-Leaver Lifecycle with AI
Pre-Read & Action Brief for the IFS HR Organisation

1. The Current State

Every hr function runs on the same lifecycle: joiner, then mover, then leaver. The Joiner-Mover-Leaver process is the operational backbone. And right now, it is broken at every stage.

Joiner is inefficient. 44-day time-to-fill, mostly screening and scheduling.

Mover is structurally neglected. Internal moves require weeks of admin across 8+ systems.

Leaver is reactive. Exit interviews after the resignation letter.

2. How to Think About the Future of HR

The Joiner-Mover-Leaver lifecycle forces specificity. Not "AI will transform hr" but exactly how each stage changes when AI agents handle the operational layer end-to-end.

The Joiner: From Job Descriptions to Skills Matching

The job description is a fiction: a wish list describing the last person in the role. AI platforms like Eightfold have indexed 1.6 billion career profiles and match on skills, not titles. For IFS, hiring across 6 countries and multiple technical domains (Go, TypeScript, React, event-driven architecture), skills-based matching is transformative. Conversational AI (Paradox/Olivia) handles screening and scheduling at scale. Onboarding shifts from checklist to orchestration: AI provisions 15 systems, sequences training, assigns mentors, and tracks milestones. By day 90, the new hire has been productively supported — not abandoned.

The Mover: The Biggest Unlock in HR

Employees who make internal moves stay nearly twice as long (7.4 vs 4.1 years). Yet internal moves at a company like IFS — with entities across multiple countries, different employment contracts, and complex ERP/cost centre structures — are administratively painful. An internal move triggers: contract amendment, cost centre remapping, payroll adjustments, benefits re-enrollment, travel policy updates, reporting line changes, and systems access reconfiguration. AI agents collapse this to hours. Platforms like Gloat and Fuel50 add the talent marketplace layer: surfacing opportunities to employees before they start browsing LinkedIn.

The Leaver: From Reactive to Predictive

Predictive attrition models flag flight risk 3–6 months ahead with up to 95% accuracy. At IFS, where losing a senior engineer or a domain expert in aerospace MRO costs 6–12 months of productivity replacement, early warning is invaluable. AI agents trigger retention interventions: compensation review, opportunity surfacing, or manager prompts. When someone does leave, AI captures institutional knowledge through structured interviews — making it searchable. The cost of attrition drops not because fewer people leave, but because departures are anticipated, managed, and their knowledge is preserved.

JML as a Continuous Loop

In the AI-native model, the boundaries dissolve. An internal move is a mini-join with its own onboarding. A leaver's knowledge persists in the system. Boomerang hires are recognised instantly. The HR function stops being an event processor and becomes a flow manager. The org chart becomes a skills graph, the headcount plan becomes a capability plan, and everything periodic becomes continuous.

3. Challenging Questions for the Room

These are not abstract thought experiments. They are the outcomes your organisation needs to engineer in the next 12 to 24 months.

Joiner

  • How do we make the entire IFS hiring process — from requisition to day-90 productive employee — run without a single recruiter manually screening a CV, scheduling an interview, or sending an onboarding checklist?
  • What has to be true for a new IFS engineer in Sri Lanka to have the same seamless, AI-orchestrated onboarding experience as one in Sweden — with AI handling all entity-specific employment and compliance differences?

Mover

  • How do we make internal mobility at IFS frictionless — where an approved role change triggers automatic contract amendment, cost centre remapping, payroll adjustment, and systems re-provisioning across all entities in under 48 hours, with zero HR ops involvement?
  • If AI removes all admin friction from internal moves, does mobility actually increase at IFS — or is the real blocker that managers won't release their best people?

Leaver

  • How do we achieve predictive attrition modelling that flags flight risk across IFS's 7,000 employees 3–6 months ahead — using engagement data, manager changes, compensation position, and peer departures — without any HRBP manually monitoring?
  • When AI captures a departing IFS engineer's institutional knowledge (key processes, system quirks, undocumented workarounds) and makes it searchable, does 'irreplaceable' stop being a thing?

North Star

  • If we go from ~75 to 30–40 in HR for a 7,000-person company, what are the 5 roles that remain — and are they all strategic (workforce planning, culture, org design) rather than operational?
  • How do we manage the transition ethically — is there a role for displaced HR ops staff in the new AI-native org, or do we invest in reskilling them for entirely different functions?
  • If HR administration becomes invisible, how does the CHRO demonstrate strategic value to the IFS board — and does the CHRO role itself need to be redefined?

5. Hands-On Automation Exercises

Do at least one before the session. Do all four if you want to arrive with a working prototype, not an opinion.

1. The Internal Move Workflow Automator

Document every step triggered when someone changes role internally at IFS: contract amendment, cost centre, payroll, benefits, reporting lines, systems access. Time each step. Calculate total elapsed time. Then build the AI version: design a workflow (in Make or Zapier) that triggers each step automatically from a single 'approved move' event. Target: under 48 hours end-to-end.

Output: A working automated internal move workflow with measured time comparison against today's manual process.

Tools: Make/Zapier, HRIS APIs, DocuSign API, Claude · Effort: 90 min

2. The Skills Graph Builder

Pick a team of 15 IFS employees. Use AI to analyse their LinkedIn profiles, internal project history, and learning records to build a skills graph. Then take 3 open roles and match internal candidates by skills, not current position. How many matches would you never have considered?

Output: A visual skills graph for 15 employees with AI-matched internal candidates for 3 open roles.

Tools: Claude, LinkedIn data, internal HR data, HTML visualisation · Effort: 60 min

3. The Predictive Attrition Prototype

Take anonymised data for 50–100 IFS leavers from the past year: tenure, role changes, compensation band, manager changes, engagement scores. Ask AI to find patterns and build a flight-risk model. Run it against the current population. Compare to what HRBPs know intuitively.

Output: A working flight-risk model with predictions for current employees and a comparison to HRBP intuition.

Tools: Claude, anonymised HR data, Python · Effort: 90 min

4. The Employee Query AI Agent

Compile IFS's top 100 employee HR questions (leave policy, benefits, expense processes, onboarding FAQs). Build an AI agent using Claude that can answer them accurately and conversationally. Test it against 20 real employee queries. Measure accuracy and response time vs. your current HR helpdesk.

Output: A working AI HR agent with measured accuracy and speed benchmarks against the current helpdesk.

Tools: Claude API, HR policy documents, Python/HTML · Effort: 2 hours

6. What to Research Before the Session

Four tasks to ground the discussion in your reality.

1. Know Your JML Numbers

Pull your actuals: time-to-fill, cost-per-hire, percentage of roles filled internally, time for an internal move to be processed, and regrettable attrition rate. If you don't have some of these, that's a finding — it tells you where your people data has blind spots.

2. Map Your Mover Friction

Talk to someone who recently changed roles internally at IFS. How long from approval to fully operational? What was painful? How many systems needed updating? The answers tell you where AI agents would create the most value.

3. Explore One Platform

Spend 20 minutes on Gloat or Visier. Gloat shows you the future of internal mobility. Visier shows you the future of people analytics. Both represent transformative capabilities for IFS's scale.

4. Ask the Uncomfortable Question

Ask your CHRO: 'If AI handled all JML administration — recruiting, contracts, payroll, benefits, onboarding, offboarding — what would our HR team actually spend their time on?' If the answer is clear, you're ahead. If there's a pause, that's the conversation.

The organisations that win the talent war will not be the ones with the best recruiters. They will be the ones where joining is frictionless, moving is encouraged, and leaving is predicted before it happens.
