Rethinking the Discover-Build-Scale Pipeline with AI-for-AI
Pre-Read & Action Brief for the IFS AI Product Organisation

1. The Current State

Every ai product unit (nexus black & ifs.ai) function runs on the same lifecycle: discover, then build, then scale. The Discover-Build-Scale process is the operational backbone. And right now, it is broken at every stage.

Discover is inefficient. Each Nexus Black engagement starts from scratch — bespoke scoping, custom models, unique infra.

Build is structurally neglected. AI engineers manually tune models per customer and per use case.

Scale is reactive. Scaling from POC to production requires re-engineering.

Traditional Discover-Build-Scale

  • Seamless POC-to-production pipeline via IFS.ai platform
  • AI readiness assessment automated: data quality scoring, infrastructure grading, use case feasibility

2. How to Think About the Future of AI Product Unit (Nexus Black & IFS.ai)

The Discover-Build-Scale lifecycle forces specificity. Not "AI will transform ai product unit (nexus black & ifs.ai)" but exactly how each stage changes when AI agents handle the operational layer end-to-end.

The Discover Stage: From Bespoke Scoping to Pattern-Matched Use Cases

Nexus Black's power is its co-creation model — deep engagement with customers on complex industrial AI. But discovery is expensive: each engagement requires solution architects to understand the customer's operations, data landscape, and objectives from scratch. AI transforms discovery by pattern-matching: a new utility customer's challenges map to templates from prior utility engagements. An aerospace MRO company's predictive maintenance needs are 70% similar to what's been built before. AI-powered readiness assessments score the customer's data quality, infrastructure, and organisational capability — before a single engineer is committed. Discovery time drops from weeks to days.

The Build Stage: From Custom Models to Templated AI Harnesses

Today, every Nexus Black engagement builds custom ML pipelines. The AI-native approach: build reusable AI harnesses per industry vertical and use case type. A predictive maintenance harness for utilities is 80% the same as one for aerospace — the data connectors, model architectures, and evaluation frameworks are templated. Engineers focus on the 20% that's bespoke: customer-specific features, integration with their specific IFS Cloud configuration, and domain-specific tuning. Platforms like Weights & Biases, MLflow, and Anthropic's Claude (for AI-assisted AI development) accelerate this. The Anthropic partnership is especially strategic here — using Claude to help build and debug the industrial AI solutions that Nexus Black delivers.

The Scale Stage: From POC to Platform Feature

The productisation mandate — every successful Nexus Black solution should be refactored into IFS Cloud — is the unit's key strategic lever. AI accelerates productisation: auto-generating the API wrappers, documentation, and configuration interfaces needed to turn a bespoke solution into a platform feature. The IFS.ai Starter Pack is the delivery vehicle. The Resolve for Utilities launch demonstrates the model: domain-specific AI built by Nexus Black, deployed as a scalable IFS Cloud capability. AI-for-AI means the unit uses AI to build AI faster.

Discover-Build-Scale as a Continuous Loop

Every deployment generates operational data that improves the models. Every customer engagement adds to the template library. Every productised feature reduces the cost of the next engagement. The AI unit becomes a flywheel: the more it builds, the faster it builds, and the more valuable IFS.ai becomes as a platform. The competitive moat isn't any single AI model — it's the compounding industrial data and domain-specific patterns that no horizontal AI vendor can replicate.

3. Challenging Questions for the Room

These are not abstract thought experiments. They are the outcomes your organisation needs to engineer in the next 12 to 24 months.

Discover

  • How do we achieve instant AI readiness assessment for any prospect — where the system scores their data quality, infrastructure, and use case feasibility in hours, not weeks — so we commit engineering only to winnable engagements?
  • What has to be true for a new Nexus Black customer engagement to start with a 70% complete solution template instead of a blank page — because the industry pattern has been built before?

Build

  • How do we make Nexus Black's AI engineers 5x more productive by building reusable harnesses per vertical — so each new engagement is configuration, not construction?
  • If we use Claude to help build industrial AI solutions (AI-for-AI), how do we ensure quality and safety in safety-critical industries like aerospace and utilities where model failures have real-world consequences?

Scale

  • How do we achieve automatic productisation — where every successful Nexus Black deployment is 80% of the way to an IFS Cloud platform feature without manual re-engineering?
  • What has to be true for the IFS.ai platform to self-improve from deployed customer data — so that every new customer benefits from the models trained on all prior customers, while maintaining data privacy and security?

North Star

  • If the same 80–100 people deliver 75 use cases instead of 15, how does the go-to-market change — does Nexus Black become a sales motion or stay a strategic programme?
  • How do we price AI capabilities at IFS when the cost of delivery drops 5x but the customer value doesn't — and is this a margin expansion opportunity or a market penetration play?
  • If IFS.ai becomes the primary competitive differentiator (not ERP features), how does the AI unit's relationship with core R&D need to change — co-located, embedded, or independent?

5. Hands-On Automation Exercises

Do at least one before the session. Do all four if you want to arrive with a working prototype, not an opinion.

1. The AI Readiness Scorer

Build an interactive tool that assesses a prospect's AI readiness. Inputs: data maturity (structured/unstructured mix), infrastructure (cloud/on-prem), current analytics capability, use case specificity, and executive sponsorship. Output: a scored readiness report with recommended starting use cases and estimated time-to-value. Design it as something a Nexus Black SA could use in the first customer meeting.

Output: A deployed AI readiness assessment tool that generates scored reports — usable by the Nexus Black team in prospect conversations.

Tools: Claude, React/HTML, scoring model · Effort: 90 min

2. The Use Case Pattern Library

Catalogue every Nexus Black engagement to date. For each: industry, use case type, data requirements, model architecture, integration pattern, and outcomes achieved. Use AI to cluster them into reusable templates. Build a searchable internal tool where engineers can query: 'Show me all predictive maintenance templates for utilities with SCADA data.'

Output: A searchable Nexus Black pattern library that accelerates new engagement scoping from weeks to hours.

Tools: Claude, engagement documentation, HTML/JavaScript · Effort: 2 hours

3. The Productisation Pipeline

Take one successful Nexus Black bespoke solution. Use AI to auto-generate: the API specification, configuration interface mock-up, documentation, and an IFS Cloud integration plan. Estimate the engineering effort to productise. Compare to building the platform feature from scratch.

Output: A complete productisation blueprint for a real Nexus Black solution — with auto-generated specs, docs, and effort estimates.

Tools: Claude, existing Nexus Black code/docs · Effort: 90 min

4. The Industrial AI Demo Builder

Build an interactive demo that shows a prospect what AI could do with their data — before they sign a Nexus Black engagement. Take a sample industrial dataset (e.g., equipment sensor data). Build a live demo that shows: anomaly detection, predictive maintenance scheduling, and cost-savings projection. Make it visually compelling enough for a C-suite presentation.

Output: An interactive AI demo that Nexus Black can show prospects: 'This is what your data could tell you' — built in hours, not weeks.

Tools: Python, Claude, HTML/ECharts, sample industrial data · Effort: 2 hours

6. What to Research Before the Session

Four tasks to ground the discussion in your reality.

1. Audit Your Engagement Reuse

Review the last 5 Nexus Black engagements. For each, estimate: what percentage of the work was genuinely novel vs. repeated from prior engagements? If reuse is below 40%, the template library doesn't exist or isn't being used — and that's the single biggest throughput lever.

2. Map Your Productisation Pipeline

Count the bespoke Nexus Black solutions that have been refactored into IFS Cloud platform features vs. those that remain one-off. If the ratio is below 1:3, the productisation pipeline is broken — and every bespoke engagement is a missed platform opportunity.

3. Explore One Platform

Spend 20 minutes on Weights & Biases or Arize AI. W&B shows you the future of ML experiment management. Arize shows you the future of production AI monitoring. Both are essential infrastructure for scaling AI.

4. Ask the Uncomfortable Question

Ask the Nexus Black leadership: 'If we could deliver 75 use cases per year instead of 15 with the same team — what changes? Does the go-to-market change? Does the pricing model change? Does the relationship with core R&D change?' If every answer is 'yes,' you've found the transformation.

The AI units that win in industrial software will not be the ones with the most data scientists. They will be the ones that turn every bespoke engagement into a platform capability — because the moat is compounding industrial intelligence, not individual models.
