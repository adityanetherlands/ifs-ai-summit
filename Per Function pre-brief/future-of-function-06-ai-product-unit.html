From Three Sales Plays to One Portfolio – Aligning Industrial Intelligence, Nexus Black & Loops

1. The Current State

The AI Business Unit houses three propositions – Industrial Intelligence, Nexus Black, and Loops – each with its own sales play, its own customer relationships, and its own definition of success. In theory, they are a portfolio. In practice, they compete for the same accounts, tell different stories to the same buyers, and build products that live outside the IFS Cloud lifecycle. The problem is not capability. It is coordination.

Alignment is undefined. There is no codified answer to which unit leads in which account. Industrial Intelligence, Nexus Black, and Loops pursue the same target accounts with separate pitches, separate qualification criteria, and separate account plans – often without knowledge of each other’s activity.

Synergies are theoretical. The combined value of the portfolio is obvious at the strategy level and invisible at the deal level. Joint solutions that would genuinely differentiate IFS in the market do not exist as packaged offerings because the teams have no mechanism to build them together.

The customer story is incoherent. A single customer may hear an Industrial Intelligence pitch about data-driven decision-making, a Nexus Black pitch about bespoke AI co-creation, and a Loops pitch about workflow automation – from three different teams, in three different quarters, with no connecting thread. The customer experiences three vendors, not one.

Products do not scale into IFS Cloud. Bespoke Nexus Black engagements remain standalone. Loops capabilities sit adjacent to, rather than inside, the IFS Cloud lifecycle. The result: customers do not get a consistent lifecycle experience and IFS does not get compounding platform value.

Siloed Portfolio (Current State)

  • Each unit maintains independent account lists, qualification criteria, and sales plays with no shared view
  • Joint solutions exist as conference-slide concepts, not as priced, packaged market offerings
  • Customer-facing teams cannot articulate when Industrial Intelligence leads vs. Nexus Black vs. Loops – and neither can the customer
  • Bespoke AI solutions built by Nexus Black remain one-off deliverables, never refactored into IFS Cloud
  • Loops capabilities are positioned alongside IFS Cloud rather than embedded within the lifecycle experience
  • Internal teams compete for the same budget line in the same account, eroding margin and customer trust

Aligned Portfolio (Target State)

  • A single account intelligence model that determines which unit leads based on customer maturity, pain point, and buying stage – agreed and enforced
  • Three to five named joint solutions taken to market: packaged combinations of Industrial Intelligence + Nexus Black + Loops with defined scope, pricing, and sales plays
  • A living account narrative per tier-1 customer that every unit reads before entering the room and updates after every interaction
  • Every Nexus Black engagement architected from day one to productise into IFS Cloud – not as an afterthought but as a design constraint
  • Loops embedded as native IFS Cloud workflow orchestration – experienced by the customer as part of the platform, not an add-on
  • Shared incentives that reward portfolio-level account growth, not unit-level deal wins

2. How to Think About the Future of AI Business Unit (Nexus Black & IFS.ai)

The portfolio alignment challenge is not a strategy problem – it is an operating model problem. Not “should these three units work together” but exactly how each unit’s role, sales motion, product architecture, and customer narrative needs to change so the portfolio creates more value together than any unit can create alone.

Sales Play Alignment: Who Leads, Who Follows, and When to Hand Off

The most operationally consequential gap in the AI Business Unit is the absence of an agreed entry logic. In any given target account, who leads? The answer today is determined by who arrives first or who has the existing relationship – not by a principled assessment of what the customer actually needs. The aligned model starts from the customer’s position. Loops leads in accounts where the presenting problem is operational process efficiency – repetitive workflows, manual handoffs, compliance bottlenecks – because Loops opens the door to operational data that Industrial Intelligence can later instrument. Industrial Intelligence leads where the customer has invested in data infrastructure and wants to extract analytical value – predictive insight, demand sensing, asset performance – because the intelligence layer creates demand for the bespoke AI that Nexus Black delivers. Nexus Black leads where the customer has a complex, high-value industrial AI ambition that requires co-creation – problems too specific for platform AI, where domain expertise and custom modelling are the differentiator. The framework is not complicated. What is complicated is enforcing it: building the account intelligence, the handoff protocols, and the shared incentives that make the framework stick in the field.

Portfolio Synergies: From Internal Competition to Joint Solutions in Market

The three units share buyers, share accounts, and share the IFS brand – but today they compete more than they collaborate. The synergy opportunity is not abstract. It is specific and commercial. An Industrial Intelligence + Nexus Black joint solution for predictive asset management takes the platform’s data layer and layers on bespoke anomaly detection models tuned to the customer’s specific equipment fleet – neither unit can deliver this alone. A Loops + Industrial Intelligence combination that embeds real-time analytics directly into operational workflows – so the maintenance planner sees the AI recommendation inside the work order process, not in a separate dashboard – closes the gap between insight and action that customers consistently identify as the barrier to AI adoption. A three-unit play for large industrial accounts – Loops for process orchestration, Industrial Intelligence for the analytics backbone, Nexus Black for the custom AI layer – is a genuinely differentiated proposition that no ERP competitor and no horizontal AI vendor can replicate. These joint solutions need to be built, named, priced, and assigned dedicated sales plays. They cannot remain hypothetical combinations that the field assembles ad hoc when a deal happens to touch multiple units.

Account Narrative: One Customer, One Story, Every Interaction

The most damaging consequence of the siloed operating model is the fragmented customer experience. When three units enter the same account independently, the customer does not hear three complementary messages – they hear three competing ones. Worse, each unit may make commitments or set expectations that conflict with what another unit has already said. The corrective is structural, not motivational: a living account narrative document for every tier-1 account. The narrative captures the customer’s stated problems, the IFS story that has been built over time, which units have been in the room, what has been promised, and what the next logical step is. Every unit reads it before every customer interaction. Every unit updates it after. When the Industrial Intelligence team presents the data platform, their narrative sets up the Nexus Black engagement that will deliver the first bespoke AI use case. When Nexus Black closes a deployment, their narrative seeds the Loops conversation that will embed the AI output into the customer’s daily operational workflow. The story compounds across interactions – it does not restart every time a different team walks in.

Product Scalability: Building for the IFS Cloud Lifecycle, Not Alongside It

The unit’s most significant long-term problem is that its products do not feel like IFS Cloud. Nexus Black solutions are delivered as bespoke, standalone implementations. Loops capabilities live adjacent to the platform. Industrial Intelligence features are experienced as add-ons rather than native capabilities. The customer experience is fragmented: they buy IFS Cloud for the lifecycle – from plan to operate – and then bolt on AI capabilities that do not share the same UX, the same data model, or the same administration layer. The target state is architectural, not cosmetic. Every Nexus Black engagement must be designed from the outset with a productisation constraint: the bespoke solution must be built on IFS Cloud APIs, conform to IFS Cloud UX patterns, and be architecturally ready to refactor into a platform feature. Loops must be embedded within the IFS Cloud lifecycle – not as a separate application but as the orchestration layer inside existing workflows. Industrial Intelligence must surface its analytics inside IFS Cloud screens, not in standalone dashboards. When the customer experiences AI through IFS, it should feel like IFS Cloud got smarter – not like three new products were attached to it.

North-Star Target
From three units selling separately → one portfolio selling a compounding industrial intelligence platform inside IFS Cloud
Joint pipeline contribution: from <15% to >50%. Productisation into IFS Cloud: from <30% to >80%.

The north star is not unit-level revenue growth. It is portfolio-level account depth – the number of target accounts where the customer buys across all three capabilities because the combined offering solves problems no single unit addresses alone, and because every capability is experienced as native IFS Cloud. The measure of success: how many accounts could not remove any one unit without losing the value of the other two.

3. Challenging Questions for the Room

These are not abstract thought experiments. They are the outcomes your organisation needs to engineer in the next 12 to 24 months.

Alignment

  • How do we build and enforce an account entry framework – where the customer’s maturity, pain point, and buying stage determine which unit leads – so that parallel pursuit of the same account by competing units is eliminated, and every tier-1 account has an agreed lead unit with a sequenced handoff plan?
  • What has to be true for Loops to systematically open doors that Industrial Intelligence and Nexus Black walk through – where a workflow automation engagement creates the operational data foundation and the customer relationship that makes an Industrial Intelligence or Nexus Black conversation a natural next step, not a cold start?

Synergies

  • Which three joint solutions – specific combinations of Industrial Intelligence, Nexus Black, and Loops capabilities – represent the largest near-term revenue opportunity, and what would it take to name them, price them, build dedicated sales plays, and take them to market within two quarters?
  • How do we change the incentive structure so that a Nexus Black lead who identifies an Industrial Intelligence opportunity in their account is rewarded as strongly as if they closed a Nexus Black deal – because today, unit-level targets actively punish collaboration?

Narrative

  • How do we build a living account narrative system – where every unit reads the shared story before entering the room and updates it after – so that the twentieth customer touchpoint builds on the first nineteen, regardless of which unit’s team was present?
  • If a tier-1 customer has received conflicting messages from two units in the same quarter, who owns the recovery – and what is the explicit protocol for resetting the narrative, re-establishing trust, and presenting a unified IFS vision?

North Star

  • If the AI Business Unit achieves >50% joint pipeline and >80% IFS Cloud productisation, does the three-unit structure still make sense – or does the operating model need to evolve toward a single, integrated industrial intelligence organisation with functional specialisations rather than separate P&Ls?
  • How do we price joint solutions when the combined value genuinely exceeds the sum of parts – and does IFS have a commercial model that captures portfolio-level value, or does the current unit-by-unit pricing force customers to evaluate each piece in isolation?
  • If every AI capability is experienced as native IFS Cloud, how does the AI Business Unit’s relationship with core R&D need to change – shared architecture governance, embedded product teams, or a unified development lifecycle?

5. Hands-On Automation Exercises

Do at least one before the session. Do all four if you want to arrive with a working model of portfolio alignment, not an opinion about it.

1. The Account Entry Sequencer

Build an interactive decision tool that takes account-level inputs – industry, operational maturity, current IFS footprint, data readiness, primary pain point, and existing unit relationships – and outputs the recommended sales play: which unit leads, in what order the others are introduced, and what the handoff triggers are between them. Test it against 10 real accounts from your current pipeline. Where the recommended sequence matches what is happening, the framework is validated. Where it diverges, you have found the alignment gap.

Output: A working sales play sequencer that gives every account team a data-driven answer to “who leads here and when do the others enter” – testable against live pipeline today.

Tools: Claude, React/HTML, CRM account data · Effort: 90 min

2. The Joint Solution Packager

Select three cross-unit combinations: Industrial Intelligence + Nexus Black, Loops + Industrial Intelligence, and all three together. For each: define the target buyer persona, the combined use case, the value proposition that neither unit can deliver alone, a pricing structure, and a two-page customer-facing pitch document. Use AI to draft the customer materials and stress-test them: for each combination, identify one real account in your pipeline where the joint solution would be more compelling than the single-unit pitch currently being delivered.

Output: Three packaged joint solution propositions – complete with buyer profile, combined value proposition, pricing logic, and customer-ready pitch materials – validated against real accounts.

Tools: Claude, unit positioning documents, HTML/JavaScript · Effort: 2 hours

3. The Account Narrative Audit

Pick five tier-1 target accounts. For each, reconstruct the complete history of IFS interactions across all three units over the past 12 months: every meeting, every proposal, every commitment. Use AI to analyse the narrative thread – where it is consistent, where it contradicts itself, and what the customer’s likely understanding of IFS is based on what they have heard. Then write the corrected narrative for each account: the single, coherent story that every unit should tell going forward, with explicit guidance on how each unit’s next conversation builds on the last.

Output: A narrative audit for five accounts – showing where the story holds, where it breaks, and a corrected unified narrative per account ready to brief into the field.

Tools: Claude, CRM notes, meeting records · Effort: 90 min

4. The IFS Cloud Integration Blueprint

Take one successful Nexus Black bespoke deployment and one Loops capability. For each, design the productisation path into IFS Cloud: the API surface, the UX integration points within existing IFS Cloud screens, the shared data model, and the administration experience. Mock up what the customer would see – AI-powered insight appearing inside IFS Cloud, not in a separate window. Estimate the engineering effort to ship it as a native capability versus the current bolt-on delivery.

Output: An IFS Cloud integration blueprint for two AI capabilities – with UX mock-ups showing native platform experience, architectural requirements, and effort estimates for productisation.

Tools: Python, Claude, HTML/ECharts, IFS Cloud documentation · Effort: 2 hours

6. What to Research Before the Session

Four tasks to ground the discussion in your reality.

1. Map Your Account Collisions

Pull the last 12 months of activity across Industrial Intelligence, Nexus Black, and Loops in your top 20 target accounts. For each: how many units have been active, is there a shared account plan, and have the messages been consistent? If more than a third show parallel or conflicting unit-level activity with no coordination, the alignment problem is structural – and every uncoordinated touchpoint makes IFS harder for the customer to buy from.

2. Count Your Joint Solutions

List every deal in the current pipeline where more than one unit has contributed to qualification, solutioning, or pricing. If joint opportunities represent less than 20% of total AI unit pipeline, the synergy is almost entirely unrealised – and the portfolio is being sold as three separate products to buyers who would pay more for an integrated one.

3. Explore One Platform Experience

Spend 20 minutes on ServiceNow’s Now Assist or Palantir’s AIP. Both demonstrate what it looks like when AI is native to the platform experience – not a separate product the customer accesses through a different interface. Ask yourself: if an IFS Cloud customer experienced Industrial Intelligence and Loops this way, would they still think of them as add-ons?

4. Ask the Uncomfortable Question

Ask each unit leader separately: ‘In our top 10 accounts, what are the other two units doing right now – and does their current customer conversation support or contradict yours?’ If the answers are vague, inconsistent, or reveal genuine conflicts, you have found the transformation. A portfolio that cannot answer this question across its own units is not a portfolio – it is three products sharing a logo.

The industrial AI portfolios that win will not be the ones with the strongest individual unit. They will be the ones where intelligence, bespoke AI, and workflow orchestration arrive in every account as a single compounding story – delivered as native platform experience – because the customer cannot buy the insight without the platform, cannot act on the platform without the workflow, and cannot get the bespoke layer without the data foundation the other two provide.
