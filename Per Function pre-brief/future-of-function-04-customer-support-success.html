Future of Customer Support & Success

What if your entire support operation delivered 70-80% resolution without human intervention, while CSMs focused exclusively on retention and expansion?

1. The Shift

The old model of customer support is structurally broken. Klarna’s AI chatbot handled two-thirds of customer service chats in its first month, equivalent to 700 full-time agents, reducing resolution time from 11 minutes to 2 minutes. But Klarna learned the hard way: automating everything at once collapsed quality. By 2025, they reversed course and rehired humans. The lesson isn’t binary-it’s that support must fundamentally restructure.

Intercom’s Fin AI agent, powered by Claude, achieves 51% average resolution rates across their customer base, with enterprise customers like Lightspeed hitting 65% autonomous resolution and closing 31% more conversations daily per agent. Sharesies achieved 70% resolution in 12 weeks; Hospitable is at 60% while handling 90% of incoming conversations automatically. These aren’t replacements-they’re force multipliers that free humans for genuinely complex work.

Sierra.ai, founded by Bret Taylor and Clay Bavor, has grown from $4.5 billion to $10 billion valuation in 12 months by building AI agents that integrate with enterprise systems, knowledge bases, and compliance frameworks. Their customers-including WeightWatchers, Sonos, SiriusXM-deploy a single agent across chat, SMS, WhatsApp, email, voice, and ChatGPT. The pattern is clear: agentic AI delivers 70-85% autonomous resolution versus 40-60% for traditional chatbots and 20-40% for RAG-only assistants.

The fundamental shift: support is evolving from reactive firefighting to predictive intelligence. AI handles the operational layer end-to-end. Humans focus on the exceptions, the relationships, and the growth.

2. Your Pre-Summit Homework

Three things to bring to the session. Not to present, but to think about. Write them down. Short answers are fine. What matters is that you’ve actually wrestled with them.

1. A bold vision for 1 January 2027

If you fast-forwarded 20 months, what does your customer support and success function look like? Not incremental. Drastically different. What disappears? What’s new? What does “a support team” even mean? Write the headline version: if someone wrote a profile of your function in January 2027, what would it say?

2. The case for transformation

Why does your customer support and success function need to transform, and why now? Not “AI is important” generically. The specific commercial, competitive, or structural reason transformation is non-negotiable for you. What breaks if you stay as you are? If you can’t articulate this in two sentences, your team won’t follow you.

3. The KPIs that matter in the new era

What are the 2-3 metrics that tell you customer support and success is winning in an AI-native world? Deflection rate? Time-to-resolution? NRR improvement? Health-weighted retention? Something entirely new? The old KPIs of tickets processed and average handle time measure effort. What measures outcome in a world where effort is automated?

3. Directional Nudges - Sense / Decide / Execute

Sense: From Reactive Monitoring to Predictive Health Signals

What if every at-risk customer was identified 3-6 months before renewal-based on usage patterns, support ticket trends, and sentiment shifts-without a human reviewing a single health score?

This is already happening. Gainsight and Totango aggregate usage analytics, support patterns, and email sentiment into customer health scores that flag churn risk months in advance. Pylon and similar platforms achieve AI ticket deflection rates of 40-60%, with best-in-class implementations reaching 85% of routine questions. The structural change is profound: the CSM stops guessing and starts intervening surgically.

What changes: Customer success shifts from gut-driven relationship management to data-driven early warning. AI continuously monitors usage drop-offs, support frequency spikes, and negative sentiment-flagging accounts before the customer even knows they’re at risk. The CSM’s job becomes intervention design, not account monitoring.

Decide: From Escalation Roulette to Intelligent Routing

What if every support ticket was diagnosed, routed to the right specialist, and pre-loaded with full context before a human ever opened it?

AI can inspect customer configurations, system logs, and historical interactions to pre-diagnose issues with accuracy that exceeds human L1 agents. When escalation is needed, the customer never repeats themselves-the full context travels with the ticket. Platforms like Zendesk AI and Ada accomplish this today. First-contact resolution rates improve from 45% to 70-80%. MTTR (mean time to resolve) drops from 4.2 days to under 4 hours.

What changes: Support escalation becomes intelligent routing, not blind handoffs. AI doesn’t just route-it pre-diagnoses and provides the next agent with a complete picture. The ticket becomes a conversation that doesn’t restart at every handoff.

Execute: From Reactive Support to Proactive Knowledge Management

What if every resolved ticket automatically improved the knowledge base, which then prevented the next 10 similar tickets from reaching any agent?

AI can extract problem-solution pairs from resolved tickets, generate knowledge base articles, identify gaps, and update documentation continuously. Guru and similar platforms surface verified answers in the flow of work. The feedback loop is self-reinforcing: lower ticket volume frees CSMs for retention and growth; growth activity surfaces new support needs that AI handles automatically; the support organization improves without adding people.

What changes: The knowledge base transforms from a static graveyard of outdated articles to a living, self-improving system. AI-generated content reduces the expertise burden on humans while improving accuracy and coverage. Support becomes easier the more it operates.

The Loop Closes

In the AI-native support model, every interaction creates data. Every resolved ticket trains the knowledge base. Every knowledge base improvement reduces future tickets. Lower volume frees humans for higher-value work. Growth and expansion activity generates new support requirements that AI handles. The support org gets better and faster without headcount growth-and often with headcount reduction.

North Star Target

Shift from 400→250 people. AI resolves 70-80% of tickets at first contact without human involvement. CSMs increase from 15-20 customers per person to 40-50. Support quality and CSAT improve despite smaller headcount. L1 support largely disappears; L2 focuses on complex, multi-system issues; CSMs focus on strategic account health and expansion.

5. Hands-On Exercises

Do at least one before the session. These give first-hand experience of AI-native support operations.

Exercise 1: The AI Support Agent Prototype  ·  30 min

Export your top 50 most common support tickets (anonymized). Feed them plus your product documentation into Claude. Build a prototype AI support agent that can answer these questions. Test it against 20 new tickets from last month. Measure: autonomous resolution rate, accuracy vs. human answer, response time.

Output: A working AI support agent prototype with measured performance against real ticket data.

Tools: Claude API, your ticket exports, product documentation, Python/HTML

Exercise 2: The Customer Health Dashboard  ·  60 min

Pull data for 20 of your at-risk customers: usage metrics, ticket volume/severity trends, NPS, email sentiment, renewal dates. Feed into Claude to generate a health score model. Build an interactive dashboard showing health trajectory, risk flags, and AI-recommended interventions for each account. Identify which accounts need CSM outreach this week.

Output: An interactive customer health dashboard with AI-generated risk scores and intervention recommendations.

Tools: Python, Claude, your CRM/support data, HTML/ECharts

Exercise 3: The Self-Maintaining Knowledge Base  ·  60 min

Take 100 resolved support tickets. Feed into Claude and ask it to: extract problem-solution pairs, generate knowledge base articles in your existing structure, identify duplicates in your current KB, flag gaps where no article exists. Design a pipeline that could run continuously on new tickets. Estimate coverage improvement.

Output: AI-generated knowledge base articles from real tickets + pipeline design for continuous self-maintenance.

Tools: Claude API, your ticket exports, Markdown/HTML
