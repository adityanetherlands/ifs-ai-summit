Future of Marketing

AI-native operations, content at scale, personalization as the default
Pre-read & inspiration brief for IFS AI Leadership Summit

The Shift Homework Nudges Companies Hands-On Resources

1. The Shift

Klarna cut its marketing agency spend by 25%, halved its marketing team from 200 to 100, and saved $10 million annually while increasing campaign output. AI-powered images that took six weeks now take seven days. 87% of employees use generative AI daily. The result: more campaigns, more creative assets, less spend.

Coca-Cola committed $1.1 billion to AI-powered marketing and built Fizzion, an intelligent creative platform that produces content ten times faster across languages and regions. Their AI-generated holiday ads scored 5.9 out of 6 in blind consumer testing among the highest-tested ads in company history.

Duolingo declared "AI-first" in 2024. Same team. Four to five times the content output. Revenue forecast crossed $1 billion. Their rule: no feature ships without asking "Can AI eliminate this function entirely?"

Three companies, three approaches, one pattern: the bottleneck in marketing was never creativity or strategy. It was production speed and personalisation at scale. AI removes both constraints simultaneously, and the organisations that move first are pulling away.

2. Your Pre-Summit Homework

Three things to bring to the session. Not to present, but to think about. Write them down. Short answers are fine. What matters is that you've actually wrestled with them.

1. A bold vision for 1 January 2027

If you fast-forwarded 8 months, what does your marketing function look like? Not incremental. Drastically different. What's disappeared? What's new? What does "a marketing team" even mean? Write the headline version: if someone wrote a profile of your function in January 2027, what would it say?

2. The case for transformation

Why does your marketing function need to transform, and why now? Not "AI is important" generically. The specific commercial, competitive, or structural reason transformation is non-negotiable for you. What breaks if you stay as you are? If you can't articulate this in two sentences, your team won't follow you.

3. The KPIs that matter in the new era

What are the 2-3 metrics that tell you marketing is winning in an AI-native world? Pipeline influenced per marketer? Content velocity? Personalisation depth? SQL-to-close improvement? Creative quality per dollar? The old KPIs of output, reach, and impressions measure effort. What measures outcome in a world where effort is automated?

3. Directional Nudges: Sense - Decide - Execute

Sense: From Quarterly Reports to Continuous Market Intelligence

What if your marketing intelligence was continuous, autonomous, and more current than any analyst could produce, and no human touched the data?

Platforms like 6sense and Metadata.io already monitor intent signals across the web, identifying in-market accounts before they raise their hand. Attribution platforms automatically connect every touchpoint (email, ad, event, content) to pipeline and revenue in real-time. Customer feedback platforms synthesise support tickets, reviews, and social mentions into market-level insights within hours.

The structural change: sensing shifts from a team of analysts preparing decks to an always-on intelligence layer. Marketers shift from compiling reports to interrogating data. The question changes from "What happened last quarter?" to "What should we do about what's happening right now?"

Decide: From Campaign Calendars to Dynamic Optimisation

What if every campaign decision (channel mix, budget allocation, messaging, creative variant) was informed by real-time performance data and AI-modelled scenarios before launch?

Platforms like Metadata.io and Mutiny autonomously test creative variants, channel mixes, and audience segments, running dozens of experiments in parallel. Claude and similar reasoning models synthesise competitive reports, audience research, and campaign performance data into strategic recommendations in minutes. Marketing automation platforms now include AI-powered next-best-action engines that recommend which channel, message, and offer will resonate most with each prospect.

The structural change: campaign optimisation becomes algorithmic instead of manual. The "decision meeting" transforms: instead of debating what to test, teams debate what the data is telling them and why the AI recommendation might miss something human judgement catches.

Execute: From Event Logistics to Content Amplification

What if every customer interaction automatically became a content asset (case study, quote, social post, video snippet) all within 48 hours, with zero production overhead?

Event transcripts and customer interactions feed into synthesis engines that automatically produce session summaries, customer quote extracts, case study outlines, social posts, and email sequences. Video platforms with AI editing generate short-form content for each talk within hours of recording. AI content distribution platforms optimise timing and messaging per platform and audience segment.

The structural change: event execution shifts from logistical to strategic. Teams shrink but output explodes (50+ content pieces per event versus 5-8 today). The survivors are strategists and storytellers, not operators.

The Loop Closes

In the AI-native marketing function, sensing is continuous, deciding is rapid, and executing is autonomous. The marketing calendar dissolves into a continuous optimisation loop. The CMO's job shifts from "Did we hit our number?" to "Are we capturing every signal and acting on it instantly?"

Thought Exercise

How might you run your marketing function with less than half its current footprint? Not through layoffs, but by eliminating the work that AI makes redundant. What disappears entirely? What gets radically faster? And what new roles emerge that nobody is doing today?

5. Hands-On Exercises

Do at least one before the session. These are designed to give you first-hand experience of what AI-native marketing feels like - not just an opinion about it.

Exercise 1: The Vertical Content Engine

30 min

Pick one topic your organisation would market. Use Claude to produce: one long-form article for industry vertical A, one for vertical B, 6 LinkedIn posts (2 per vertical), and 3 email subject lines per vertical. Measure elapsed time. Count total assets created. Compare to your content team's typical output.

Output: 18+ pieces of vertical-specific content from one brief.
Tools: Claude (claude.ai), your brand guidelines

Exercise 2: The Intent-to-Pipeline Dashboard

60 min

Export data from your marketing automation and CRM. Build an interactive dashboard showing: campaign ROI by source, conversion velocity by stage, and AI-recommended next actions per account. The goal is to see what an always-on marketing intelligence layer looks like.

Output: An interactive attribution dashboard connecting spend to pipeline with AI insights.
Tools: Claude, Python or Excel, CRM/marketing data exports

Exercise 3: The Event Content Accelerator

90 min

Take a recording or transcript from your last event. Use Claude to produce: session summaries, customer quote extracts, 10 LinkedIn posts, a newsletter draft, and a highlights one-pager. Compare to your current post-event workflow - both in time and volume of assets.

Output: A complete post-event content package (8-10 pieces) produced in under 90 minutes.
Tools: Claude, event transcripts, brand templates
