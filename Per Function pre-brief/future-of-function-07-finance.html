Future of Finance

When AI collapses the data supply chain from transaction to boardroom

1. The Shift

JPMorgan deployed COIN (Contract Intelligence) to automate commercial loan agreements. The result: 360,000 hours of manual work eliminated annually. One contract that used to take 360,000 hours of legal review time now takes seconds. Not through layoffs · through reimagining what the finance function actually does.

Intuit built AI-powered bookkeeping that handles invoice-to-ledger reconciliation automatically. Their financial close, which took weeks of manual reconciliation across thousands of small businesses, now happens in hours. Thousands of accountants went from reconciling transactions to reviewing exceptions flagged by AI.

Stripe automated revenue recognition for SaaS companies. Complex subscription deals that require specialist judgment to classify (recurring vs. one-time, performance obligations across multiple periods) are now handled by AI that reads contracts, extracts terms, and applies ASC 606 rules. The revenue accountant moves from classifying deals to auditing AI decisions.

BlackLine reported that enterprise finance teams using their AI-powered close platform compress month-end close from 10-15 days to 3-4 days. Manual reconciliations that consumed 40% of accounting time are now autonomous. Finance staff who used to spend weeks on month-end now spend days on analysis.

One pattern: finance functions aren’t shrinking through headcount cuts. They’re restructuring. The transactional accounting layer becomes autonomous. The humans who remain shift from data mechanics to decision support · asking better questions about cash, capital allocation, and risk instead of compiling spreadsheets.

2. Your Pre-Summit Homework

Three things to bring to the session. Not to present, but to think about. Write them down. Short answers are fine. What matters is that you've actually wrestled with them.

1. A bold vision for 1 January 2027

If you fast-forwarded 20 months, what does your Finance function look like? Not incremental. Drastically different. What's disappeared? What's new? Write the headline version: if someone wrote a profile of your function in January 2027, what would it say?

2. The case for transformation

Why does your finance function need to transform, and why now? Not "AI is important" generically. The specific commercial, competitive, or structural reason transformation is non-negotiable for you. What breaks if you stay as you are? If you can't articulate this in two sentences, your team won't follow you.

3. The KPIs that matter in the new era

What are the 2-3 metrics that tell you Finance is winning in an AI-native world? Days to close? Forecast accuracy? Cost per invoice? Analyst output per head? Exceptions rate? Cash conversion cycle? The old KPIs of headcount and transaction volume measure effort. What measures outcome in a world where effort is automated?

3. Directional Nudges: Sense / Decide / Execute

Sense: From Monthly Reporting to Continuous Financial Intelligence

What if your CFO had real-time visibility into cash position, revenue trajectory, and financial anomalies · updated daily without a single analyst manually gathering data?

This is already production-grade. Treasury platforms like Kyriba use AI to monitor cash positions across hundreds of bank accounts, forecast liquidity in real-time, and flag opportunities to optimize working capital. AI anomaly detection systems monitor expense patterns, procurement trends, and revenue velocity · surfacing deviations before they hit the monthly report.

The structural change: the finance team stops being a monthly reporting function and becomes a continuous sensing layer. The people who used to prepare board decks now monitor the continuous model and escalate only when assumptions shift or risks emerge.

Decide: From Spreadsheet Scenarios to Probabilistic Models

What if every capital allocation decision · pricing, M&A, investment, cost optimization · came with 100+ pre-modelled scenarios before the CFO team met?

Causal and Planful now let CFOs build interactive financial models where scenario analysis is instant. Change one assumption (CAC, churn, pricing), and revenue impact, margin impact, and cash impact cascade instantly. What used to take an analyst three days (model build, scenario run, sensitivity analysis) now takes 15 minutes.

The structural change: decisions compress from weeks to days. Not because judgment improves, but because the information bottleneck evaporates. When financial scenarios are instant, the limiting factor becomes debate, not data. And that debate is where the CFO should spend time.

Execute: From Quarterly Reviews to Real-Time Financial Operations

What if your financial operations · reconciliation, accrual posting, intercompany eliminations, expense approval · ran autonomously and escalated only exceptions?

BlackLine’s platform now handles daily reconciliation for multi-entity consolidations. No humans match intercompany transactions · AI does. Standard accruals (payroll, depreciation, revenue recognition for routine deals) post automatically. The accountant no longer updates reconciliation schedules; they review AI-flagged mismatches.

The structural change: financial operations become an always-on, autonomous backbone. The 50-person accounting team becomes 20 people who monitor automation and handle judgement calls. The freed capacity shifts to revenue operations, FP&A, and tax strategy.

The Loop Closes

In the AI-native finance function, sensing is continuous, not monthly. Deciding is rapid, not quarterly. Execution is autonomous, not manual. The CFO’s meeting shifts from “here’s what happened” to “here’s what’s changing and what we do about it.”

Thought Exercise

If AI handled every month-end close, every revenue recognition decision, every reconciliation, and every routine accrual - what would your finance function actually do with their time? Would you need the same headcount? If not, what capabilities would you actually expand into?

4. Tough Questions to Ask Yourself

Sense

  • How many days pass between a transaction occurring and your CFO seeing its financial impact?
  • What percentage of your accounting team’s time goes to data gathering (reconciliation, accrual posting, consolidation) vs. analysis?
  • If revenue and expense actuals fed automatically into a rolling forecast, and it updated daily, would your quarterly forecast process still exist?

Decide

  • How long does it take from “we’re considering this pricing change” to “we understand the full P&L impact”?
  • How many spreadsheets does your FP&A team maintain, and what percentage of their time goes to Excel vs. actual forecasting judgment?
  • If every capital allocation decision came with AI-modelled scenarios, would your CFO make different decisions?

Execute

  • How many people in your finance function do transactional work (reconciliations, journal entries, accrual posting) that could be automated today?
  • If your month-end close went from 15 days to 3 days, what would those freed 12 days be spent on?
  • What financial decisions are you deferring because you don’t have current data · and would real-time data change your strategy?

North Star

  • If you redesigned your finance function with AI doing all transactional work, what would the remaining team do?
  • What are the 2-3 strategic capabilities your finance team could build if they had 30% more time?
  • If finance became a decision support function instead of a reporting function, how does the CFO’s role change?

6. Hands-On Exercises

Do at least one before the session. Do all three if you want to arrive with working prototypes.

Exercise 1: The AI-Driven Month-End Close Map  ·  30 min

Take your current month-end close process. Break it into steps: reconciliation, accrual posting, intercompany eliminations, variance analysis, reporting. For each step, classify: fully automatable (reconciliations, standard journal entries), partially automatable (complex accruals with AI support), or requires human judgment.

Output: A visual process map showing the AI-native close timeline and time reduction.

Tools: Claude, current close process documentation, HTML/Markdown

Exercise 2: The Continuous Revenue Forecast Engine  ·  60 min

Build a probabilistic revenue forecast model for your business. Inputs: current ARR, pipeline data (by stage), historical conversion rates, churn patterns, and seasonal trends. Use AI to generate a rolling 12-month forecast with confidence intervals (ranges, not point estimates). Update it daily based on pipeline movement.

Output: An interactive rolling forecast dashboard with daily updates and confidence ranges.

Tools: Claude, Python or Excel, HTML/JavaScript, your financial data

Exercise 3: The Invoice Processing Prototype  ·  90 min

Take 50 sample invoices (real or anonymised). Build an AI pipeline that: reads the invoice, extracts vendor, amount, GL account, cost centre; matches it to a PO; and routes for approval based on dollar amount and policy rules. Measure accuracy against manual coding.

Output: A working invoice processing prototype with accuracy metrics and a 30/60/90-day deployment plan.

Tools: Claude API, PDF parsing, Python or Node.js, your expense data
