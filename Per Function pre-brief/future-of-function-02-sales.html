Future of Sales

What if your sales team tripled revenue per rep without hiring more people?

1. The Shift

Gong analysed over one million sales opportunities and found that teams using AI generate 77% more revenue per representative. But here's the provocative part: they also found that organisations using AI in 2024 grew revenue 29% faster than peers, yet didn't proportionally grow their sales teams. Same headcount. Radically different output.

Outreach built AI agents into their revenue workflow platform. Customers report 60% productivity gains, 20-30% improvement in lead conversion, and sales cycles shortened by 11 days. One enterprise moved from a 6-week sales cycle to 5 weeks, not through hiring, but through automating research, sequencing, and deal intelligence.

HubSpot customers deploying AI SDRs see 28% higher response rates on outreach. Personalised email sequences powered by AI achieve 29% higher open rates and 41% higher click-through rates. The pattern is consistent: AI doesn't replace reps. It removes the 60% of their week consumed by research, admin, and low-intent prospecting.

The structural shift: sales stops being a pipeline machine run by humans and becomes an intelligence system run by humans and AI in lockstep. Every rep, every day, has access to real-time account intelligence, predictive deal scoring, and automated next-best actions. The friction of research, manual prospecting, and boring follow-ups vanishes overnight.

2. Your Pre-Summit Homework

Three things to bring to the session. Not to present, but to think about. Write them down. Short answers are fine. What matters is that you've actually wrestled with them.

1. A bold vision for 1 January 2027

If you fast-forwarded 20 months, what does your sales function look like? Not incremental. Drastically different. What's disappeared? What's new? What does "a sales team" even mean? Write the headline version: if someone wrote a profile of your function in January 2027, what would it say?

2. The case for transformation

Why does your sales function need to transform, and why now? Not "AI is important" generically. The specific commercial, competitive, or structural reason transformation is non-negotiable for you. What breaks if you stay as you are? If you can't articulate this in two sentences, your team won't follow you.

3. The KPIs that matter in the new era

What are the 2-3 metrics that tell you sales is winning in an AI-native world? Revenue per rep? Cycle time? Win rate on AI-scored deals? Something entirely new? The old KPIs of activity, meetings booked, and pipeline generated measure effort. What measures outcome in a world where effort is automated?

3. Directional Nudges: Sense / Decide / Execute

Sense: From Manual Research to Continuous Account Intelligence

What if every rep walked into every first call armed with account intelligence so specific (financials, recent hiring, tech stack, pain points by role) that they knew the prospect's business better than the prospect did?

This is already happening. Gong's research shows organisations deploying AI for strategic account intelligence are 2-4x more likely to generate above-median commercial impact. The AI sifts through financial filings, job postings, earnings calls, and public signals to surface exactly what matters: budget cycles, headcount expansion, competitive displacement risk, equipment replacement cycles.

The structural change: the "sensing" layer of sales, which used to be reps spending 5+ hours/week on LinkedIn and annual reports, becomes instant. The human who remains in this layer shifts from data gathering to deal strategy. They ask better questions of the AI, not better questions of Google.

Decide: From Gut Feel to AI-Scored Pipelines

What if every deal in your pipeline came with a predicted win probability, next-best action, and a warning flag if the deal is at risk, without a single human updating a forecast?

Outreach predicts whether deals will close with 81% accuracy. When you know which deals are actually winnable, where to focus becomes obvious. A rep spending time on a 15% confidence deal could be redirected to three 70% confidence deals. That's not management. That's intelligence.

Gong found that revenue organisations with AI-native decision-making see 65% higher win rates. The bottleneck was never the rep's ability to sell. It was information clarity. When AI surfaces the signals that predict wins and losses, reps naturally focus on the deals that matter.

The structural change: the weekly deal review shifts from "how much pipeline do we have" to "which deals do we actually move this week, and what's the blockers." Forecasting becomes predictive, not aspirational.

Execute: From Activity Logging to Autonomous Engagement

What if sequences, follow-ups, and outreach ran on autopilot, while AI monitored every response, flagged buying signals, and recommended the next move before your rep had to think about it?

HubSpot customers automating outreach see 50% more leads and 30% faster conversion times. But the magic isn't scale. It's quality. Automated sequences get personalised by AI based on what the system knows about each prospect. The email isn't generic; it's specific to their industry, their role, their company's recent news.

Outreach saves sales reps 30% of their time on repetitive tasks. That's not small. That's one full day per week redirected from admin to deals.

The structural change: execution visibility becomes autonomous. AI monitors every touchpoint (email opens, engagement patterns, meeting sentiment) and escalates only what matters. The SDR or AE sees their pipeline as a living, breathing entity, not a spreadsheet to update manually.

The Loop Closes

In the AI-native sales model, sensing is continuous, deciding is rapid, executing is automated. The rep's job shifts from "run activity" to "run strategy." They negotiate deals, build relationships, and remove blockers. Everything else is handled by the system.

Thought Exercise

If AI handles prospecting research, outreach sequencing, follow-up automation, and pipeline scoring, how many more deals can 10 reps close? Or: could you do the same number of deals with 5 reps, both selling instead of researching?

4. Tough Questions to Ask Yourself

Sense

  • If AI could generate a real-time account brief in seconds, what would your reps actually research?
  • How much of your selling time is finding information versus using it to sell?
  • What happens to your SDR team when AI can research 100 prospects overnight at higher quality than manual work?

Decide

  • If every deal came with a predicted win probability and next-best action, would you still need weekly forecast calls?
  • What is your average deal cycle length today, and how much of that is reps waiting for information versus negotiating?
  • If AI flagged your at-risk deals 2 weeks before they slipped, how much revenue would you save?

Execute

  • How many reps spend more than 25% of their week on manual outreach sequencing and follow-up?
  • If AI automated 60% of repetitive sales tasks, what would your team do with that time?
  • Would your SDR-to-AE conversion rate improve if AI qualified leads with 81% accuracy instead of human gatekeepers?

North Star

  • How might you run your sales function with less than half its current footprint? Not through layoffs, but through eliminating the work that AI makes redundant. What roles disappear? What new ones emerge?
  • If revenue per rep tripled and headcount stayed flat, what are the 2-3 new capabilities your sales team needs that don't exist today?
  • If every rep had a personal AI copilot that knew their entire pipeline, competitive context, and customer health in real-time, how would your coaching and management shift?

6. Hands-On Exercises

Do at least one before the session. These are designed to give you hands-on experience of what AI-native selling feels like, not just an opinion.

Exercise 1: The AI Prospect Brief  ·  30 min

Pick 5 target accounts from your pipeline. Use Claude + web search to auto-generate a comprehensive account brief for each: company overview, recent financials, likely pain points for their industry, current tech stack, and a personalised value hypothesis for your solution. Compare to what your AEs would produce manually. Build it as a reusable prompt template.

Output: 5 AI-generated account briefs and a reusable prompt template any rep can use to generate prospect-ready intelligence in minutes.

Tools: Claude, web search, company filings

Exercise 2: The Deal Scoring Pipeline  ·  60 min

Export your last 50 deals, won and lost. Feed key attributes into Claude: deal size, sales cycle length, champion title, competitor mentioned, contract signed date, time in each stage. Ask AI to build a predictive scoring model identifying the patterns that predict wins vs. losses. Score your current open pipeline. Identify your riskiest deals.

Output: A data-backed deal scoring model with your current pipeline ranked by win probability and flagged risks.

Tools: Claude, Python or Excel, CRM exports

Exercise 3: The Automated Outreach Sequence  ·  90 min

Build an AI-powered outreach workflow: define 5 target account triggers (budget cycle, hiring spike, competitor displacement risk), use Claude API to auto-generate personalised messaging for each, and create a multi-touch sequence (email, follow-up, meeting request, LinkedIn). Test it against 20 of your real prospects. Measure reply rate vs. your current manual sequences.

Output: A working automated outreach system that generates personalised sequences without manual copywriting.

Tools: Make.com or Zapier, Claude API, email integration, CRM

Exercise 4: Rebuild Your QBR as an AI-Generated Artefact  ·  60-90 min

Stop building your Quarterly Business Review as a static 40-slide deck. Rebuild it as a live, interactive artefact. Export your CRM data: last quarter's pipeline, closed-won, closed-lost, stage conversion rates, rep-level performance. Ask Claude to build a QBR app you can click into: deal health scores, at-risk accounts, coverage ratios, forecasted next quarter, rep-level patterns. Run your next QBR from the artefact, not the slides.

Output: An interactive QBR artefact. A working app your leadership can navigate and interrogate, not a deck to sit through.

Tools: Claude, Claude artefacts, CRM export
