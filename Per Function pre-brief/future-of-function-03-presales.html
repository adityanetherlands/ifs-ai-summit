1. The Current State

Every pre-sales — technical demo’ing function runs on the same lifecycle: discover, then demo, then prove. The Discover-Demo-Prove process is the operational backbone. And right now, it is broken at every stage.

Discover is inefficient. RFP responses take 2–4 weeks with 3–5 people assembling answers from memory and old docs.

Demo is structurally neglected. Solution Architects maintain 40+ demo scripts in their heads.

Prove is reactive. POC/pilot scoping is art, not science — inconsistent across the team.

Traditional Discover-Demo-Prove

  • RFP responses take 2–4 weeks with 3–5 people assembling answers from memory and old docs
  • Demo environments manually configured for each prospect — 8–15 hours per demo
  • Discovery insights trapped in meeting notes, rarely systematically analysed
  • Solution Architects maintain 40+ demo scripts in their heads
  • POC/pilot scoping is art, not science — inconsistent across the team
  • Competitive positioning researched ad-hoc before each deal

AI-Native Discover-Demo-Prove

  • AI generates first-draft RFP responses in hours, pulling from verified knowledge base — humans review, not write
  • Demo environments auto-configured from discovery data: industry, company size, use cases
  • AI synthesises discovery calls into structured requirements with gap analysis in minutes
  • AI-generated demo scripts personalised to each prospect’s specific operational context
  • POC scoping automated: AI recommends scope, timeline, and success criteria from similar wins
  • Competitive positioning auto-generated and updated daily from market intelligence

2. How to Think About the Future of Pre-Sales

The Discover-Demo-Prove lifecycle forces specificity. Not “AI will transform pre-sales” but exactly how each stage changes when AI agents handle the operational layer end-to-end.

The Discover Stage: From Note-Taking to Insight Extraction

Discovery at IFS involves understanding complex operational environments — aerospace MRO workflows, utility grid management, manufacturing production planning. Today, a Solution Architect spends hours in discovery calls, takes notes, and manually maps requirements to IFS capabilities. AI transforms this: conversation intelligence (Gong, Chorus) transcribes and analyses every discovery call, extracts structured requirements, identifies gaps against IFS Cloud capabilities, and generates a discovery summary with recommended solution architecture. The SA stops being a note-taker and becomes a strategic advisor who walks into the next meeting with an AI-generated solution hypothesis.

The Demo Stage: From Scripted Walkthroughs to Living Experiences

IFS demos are complex — the product spans ERP, EAM, FSM, and SCM across 6 industries. A great demo requires deep product knowledge AND deep industry knowledge. AI collapses the prep: given discovery data, AI generates a demo script tailored to the prospect’s industry, use cases, and pain points. Demo environments are auto-configured with industry-relevant data (an aerospace MRO company sees aircraft fleet data, not generic manufacturing orders). Platforms like Demostack and Reprise let you create interactive, personalised demo experiences at scale. The number of SAs needed drops because each SA can deliver twice as many high-quality, personalised demos.

The Prove Stage: From POCs to Predictive Value

Proof-of-value at IFS typically means a 4–8 week pilot with significant SA investment. AI compresses this: historical win data identifies which POC scope and success criteria correlate with closed deals. AI auto-generates the POC proposal, configures the environment, and monitors success metrics automatically. Value engineering — the process of quantifying ROI for the customer’s CFO — shifts from spreadsheet-based to AI-modelled: ‘Based on 400 similar deployments, here is your expected improvement in first-time-fix rate, scheduling efficiency, and asset uptime.’ The proof becomes data-driven, not anecdotal.

Discover-Demo-Prove as a Continuous Loop

In the AI-native model, every discovery call enriches the demo library. Every demo outcome trains the recommendation engine. Every POC result improves the value model. The pre-sales organisation stops rebuilding from scratch for each deal and starts compounding knowledge. A new SA joining IFS has instant access to the collective intelligence of every deal the company has ever run — synthesised by AI into actionable playbooks, not buried in a SharePoint.

Solution Architects remain for complex, bespoke engagements — but the volume of work that requires a human drops dramatically when AI agents handle discovery analysis, demo personalization, and proof-of-value configuration end-to-end.

3. Challenging Questions for the Room

These are not abstract thought experiments. They are the outcomes your organisation needs to engineer in the next 12 to 24 months.

Discover

  • How do we make every discovery call automatically produce a structured requirements matrix, gap analysis against IFS Cloud, and recommended solution architecture — with zero manual SA effort post-call?
  • What has to be true for a new Solution Architect at IFS to walk into their first customer meeting with the same depth of knowledge as a 10-year veteran — because AI has synthesised every prior engagement in that industry?

Demo

  • How do we achieve fully personalised demos for every prospect — industry-specific data, company-specific use cases, role-specific views — without any SA spending more than 30 minutes on preparation?
  • If AI can generate a demo script, configure the environment, and even narrate the standard product walkthrough, what is the actual role of the Solution Architect in a demo — and is it closer to ‘strategic advisor’ than ‘product expert’?

Prove

  • How do we make every proof-of-value engagement outcome-predictive — where AI tells us ‘deals with this scope and these success criteria close at 78%’ — so we stop running POCs that were never going to convert?
  • What has to be true for IFS to deliver a CFO-ready ROI model in real-time during a sales meeting, personalised to the prospect’s operational data, without any value engineer touching a spreadsheet?

North Star

  • If we go from ~200 to 100–120 in pre-sales, what does the surviving team look like — and is the job title still ‘Solution Architect’ or something entirely different?
  • How do we reskill RFP analysts and demo specialists for a world where content generation is AI’s job — and human value is in strategic deal architecture and executive storytelling?
  • If AI handles 80% of RFP responses, 90% of demo prep, and all value modelling — does IFS need a separate pre-sales function at all, or does it merge into a smaller, more strategic deal team?

5. Hands-On Automation Exercises

Do at least one before the session. Do all four if you want to arrive with a working prototype, not an opinion.

1. The AI RFP Responder

Take an actual IFS RFP response from the last 6 months. Feed the original RFP questions and IFS’s knowledge base (product docs, prior responses, case studies) into Claude. Ask it to generate a complete first draft. Compare to the human version. Calculate the hours that could have been saved if AI handled the first draft and humans only reviewed and refined.

Output: A complete AI-generated RFP first draft with a time-savings analysis and quality comparison to the human version.

Tools: Claude, IFS product documentation, prior RFP responses · Effort: 45 min

2. The Discovery-to-Demo Pipeline

Record (or use a transcript from) a real IFS discovery call. Feed it into AI and ask it to: extract structured requirements, map them to IFS Cloud modules, identify gaps, and generate a personalised demo script with talking points for each module. Then auto-generate a demo data set appropriate for the prospect’s industry.

Output: A complete AI-generated demo preparation package: requirements matrix, solution map, demo script, and industry-specific data set.

Tools: Claude, Gong/Otter transcripts, IFS product docs · Effort: 60 min

3. The Prospect Value Model

Build an interactive value model that takes 5 inputs (industry, asset count, field workforce size, current first-time-fix rate, annual maintenance spend) and outputs a personalised ROI projection for IFS Cloud. Use IFS benchmark data from case studies and published metrics. Make it visual enough to show a CFO.

Output: A deployed interactive value calculator that any SE can use during a customer meeting to generate a personalised ROI model on the fly.

Tools: Claude, HTML/JavaScript, IFS benchmark data · Effort: 90 min

4. The Pre-Sales Knowledge Graph

Take 10 won IFS deals from the last year. Feed the RFPs, proposals, discovery notes, and demo feedback into AI. Ask it to build a searchable knowledge base: which requirements came up most, which demo sections resonated, which competitive displacements worked, and which objections were hardest to overcome. Deploy it as an interactive internal tool.

Output: A searchable, AI-powered pre-sales knowledge base that any SA can query: ‘Show me how we handled the FSM scheduling objection in utility deals.’

Tools: Cursor, Claude API, deal documents, HTML · Effort: 2 hours

6. What to Research Before the Session

Four tasks to ground the discussion in your reality.

1. Time Your Demo Prep

Pick the next 3 demos on your calendar. Time every minute spent on preparation: research, environment setup, script writing, data loading. Then estimate what percentage of that prep could be handled by AI with access to the discovery transcript and IFS product knowledge.

2. Audit Your RFP Process

Pull your last 5 RFP responses. Calculate elapsed time, number of people involved, and percentage of content that was net-new vs. recycled from prior responses. If reuse is under 50%, your knowledge management is the bottleneck — and the exact thing AI solves.

3. Explore One Platform

Spend 20 minutes on Demostack or Vivun. Demostack shows you the future of personalised demos. Vivun shows you the future of pre-sales intelligence. Both are used by enterprise software companies of IFS’s scale.

4. Ask the Uncomfortable Question

Ask your head of pre-sales: ‘If AI could generate the first draft of every RFP, configure every demo environment, and produce every ROI model — what would our 200-person pre-sales org actually look like?’ If the answer is ‘100 strategic deal architects,’ you’re on the right track.

The pre-sales teams that win enterprise deals will not be the ones with the most Solution Architects. They will be the ones where every demo feels like it was built for that one customer — because AI did the personalisation that used to take a team a week.
