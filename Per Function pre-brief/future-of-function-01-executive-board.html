1. The Current State

Every executive board & management function runs on the same lifecycle: sense, then decide, then execute. The Sense-Decide-Execute process is the operational backbone. And right now, it is broken at every stage.

Sense is inefficient. Monthly/quarterly reporting cycles — decisions lag reality by weeks.

Decide is structurally neglected. M&A due diligence takes 3–6 months of analyst time.

Execute is reactive. Competitive intelligence via ad-hoc analyst requests.

2. How to Think About the Future of Executive Board & Management

The Sense-Decide-Execute lifecycle forces specificity. Not “AI will transform executive board & management” but exactly how each stage changes when AI agents handle the operational layer end-to-end.

The Sense Stage: From Monthly Reports to Continuous Intelligence

IFS generates mountains of operational data across 6 verticals and 10,000+ customers. Today, this data is filtered through analysts, dashboards, and decks before reaching the executive team — a process that takes weeks and loses fidelity at every step. AI agents can continuously monitor ARR trends, pipeline velocity, customer health scores, R&D velocity, and competitive signals, surfacing only what matters and explaining why. Platforms like Glean (enterprise AI search across all internal systems), Pyramid Analytics (decision intelligence), and Anthropic’s Claude (for synthesis and scenario analysis) make this production-grade today. The executive team stops asking “what happened last quarter” and starts asking “what’s about to happen and what should we do about it.”

The Decide Stage: From Consensus Cycles to AI-Informed Conviction

Strategic decisions at IFS — pricing changes, M&A targets, market entry, resource allocation across verticals — currently require weeks of analysis, stakeholder alignment, and committee review. AI transforms this by running scenario models in real-time: “What happens to ARR if we raise prices 8% in A&D but hold manufacturing flat?” or “Model the 3-year revenue impact of acquiring a warehouse management vendor.” The Softeon acquisition analysis that took months of analyst work could be done in days with AI financial modelling. The board doesn’t decide less carefully — it decides faster and with better data.

The Execute Stage: From Cascading OKRs to Autonomous Tracking

Once a strategic decision is made, execution tracking is manual: OKRs cascade through the organisation, quarterly business reviews check progress, and strategy-to-execution gaps widen silently. AI agents can monitor execution continuously — tracking whether the six transformation strategies (owned by Raymond, Dan, Piyush, Tony, Alex, and the wider leadership) are on track, flagging blockers before they escalate, and auto-generating the status updates that currently consume hours of leadership time. The NGA programme, IFS Zero milestones, and Nexus Black delivery cadence all become continuously visible without anyone manually updating a tracker.

Sense-Decide-Execute as a Continuous Loop

In the AI-native model, the executive function stops operating in quarterly cycles. Sensing is continuous (AI monitors everything). Deciding is rapid (models are always current). Execution is tracked autonomously (AI flags deviations). The board meeting shifts from “reviewing what happened” to “debating what to do next” — because the information supply chain is instant. The executive team becomes smaller but dramatically more effective: fewer people preparing, more people deciding and acting.

3. Challenging Questions for the Room

These are not abstract thought experiments. They are the outcomes your organisation needs to engineer in the next 12 to 24 months.

Sense

  • How do we get to a state where every board meeting starts with an AI-generated brief that’s more accurate and current than anything a team of analysts could produce — and no human touched the data?
  • What has to be true for IFS’s competitive intelligence to be continuous and autonomous — monitoring every competitor move, analyst report, and customer signal — without a single strategy analyst manually compiling it?

Decide

  • How do we make M&A financial modelling a same-day exercise where AI runs 50 scenarios before lunch, so the board debates strategy instead of waiting for spreadsheets?
  • If AI can model the revenue impact of every pricing, packaging, and go-to-market decision in real-time, what is the actual role of the quarterly business review?

Execute

  • How do we achieve continuous execution tracking across all six transformation strategies without a single person manually updating a status report or OKR tracker?
  • What has to be true for the NGA programme, Nexus Black delivery pipeline, and IFS Zero milestones to be monitored and reported on autonomously — with AI escalating risks before humans notice them?

North Star

  • If we go from ~25 to 12–15 in the executive and strategy function, what are the 3 new roles that don’t exist today — and where do we find people who can do AI-augmented strategic analysis?
  • How do we reskill Chiefs of Staff and strategy analysts for a world where data preparation is no longer their job — and their value is entirely in judgement, synthesis, and stakeholder navigation?
  • If every IFS executive has a personal AI agent that knows everything about their function’s performance, pipeline, and risks — does the monthly leadership meeting even need to exist in its current form?

5. Hands-On Automation Exercises

Do at least one before the session. Do all four if you want to arrive with a working prototype, not an opinion.

1. The Auto-Generated Board Pack

Take IFS’s last quarterly board pack. Feed the raw data sources (ARR numbers, pipeline data, customer health scores, R&D velocity metrics) into Claude and ask it to generate a complete board narrative with analysis, risks, and recommendations. Compare the AI output to the human-created version. Where is it better? Where does it miss nuance? Estimate the hours saved.

Output: A complete AI-generated board narrative document that you can show side-by-side with the real one.

Tools: Claude, Excel/CSV data exports · Effort: 45 min

2. The Real-Time Competitive Radar

Pick IFS’s top 3 competitors (SAP, Oracle, ServiceNow). Build an automated workflow that monitors their press releases, job postings, pricing pages, and analyst mentions daily. Use AI to generate a weekly competitive intelligence summary with strategic implications for IFS. Set it up to run continuously.

Output: A working automated competitive intelligence pipeline that delivers weekly AI-synthesised competitor briefs.

Tools: Make/Zapier, Claude API, RSS feeds, web scraping · Effort: 90 min

3. The Scenario Modelling Engine

Take a real strategic question facing IFS (e.g., ‘What is the 3-year ARR impact of increasing prices 10% in A&D while launching a freemium tier for SMB manufacturing?’). Build an interactive scenario model using AI: define the variables, run 10 scenarios, and produce an interactive HTML report showing outcomes, sensitivities, and recommended actions.

Output: An interactive HTML scenario analysis report that a board member could use to explore trade-offs.

Tools: Claude, Python, HTML/JavaScript · Effort: 2 hours

4. The Execution Tracker Automation

Map IFS’s six transformation strategies to their key milestones. Build an automated system that pulls status data from the project management tools being used (Jira, Monday.com, etc.), runs it through AI to assess progress against plan, and generates a weekly executive summary with red/amber/green status and AI-recommended interventions. Deploy it for a 2-week trial.

Output: A working automated strategy execution dashboard that generates AI-powered weekly status reports without manual input.

Tools: Cursor, APIs (Jira/Monday.com), Claude API, HTML · Effort: 2 hours

6. What to Research Before the Session

Four tasks to ground the discussion in your reality.

1. Know Your Information Supply Chain

Map every data source that feeds into your monthly leadership meeting or board pack. Count the systems, the people who touch the data, and the elapsed time from raw data to executive insight. The number will surprise you — and it tells you exactly where AI agents create the most value.

2. Time Your Decision Cycle

Pick the last 3 strategic decisions the executive team made (pricing change, investment approval, org change). Measure the elapsed time from ‘question raised’ to ‘decision made.’ Then estimate how much of that time was spent gathering data vs. actually debating the decision.

3. Explore One Platform

Spend 20 minutes on Glean or Pyramid Analytics. Watch a demo. Read one case study. These platforms represent the production-grade state of executive AI. Start with Glean if your pain is finding information across systems. Start with Pyramid if your pain is turning data into decisions.

4. Ask the Uncomfortable Question

Ask your CEO or board chair: ‘If AI could prepare every board meeting, track every strategic initiative, and model every scenario — what would the executive team actually spend their time on?’ If the answer is clear and compelling, IFS is ahead. If there’s a long pause, that’s the conversation.

The executive teams that win the next decade will not be the ones with the most analysts. They will be the ones whose leaders spend 80% of their time on judgement and 20% on information — because AI handles the rest.
